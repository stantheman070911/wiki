# The STEPPS Framework - Six Drivers of Word of Mouth

## One-line summary
Things catch on because of the *message*, not a special messenger — and six engineerable qualities (Social Currency, Triggers, Emotion, Public, Practical Value, Stories) make any product or idea more likely to be talked about and shared.

## Context
The master framework from Jonah Berger's *Contagious*, synthesizing a decade of research on why some content spreads and most doesn't. It reframes three common beliefs:

- **Virality is made, not born.** A boring blender (Blendtec's *Will It Blend?*) and a plain cheesesteak (Barclay Prime's $100 version) both went viral because someone engineered the sharing — not because they were inherently exciting.
- **The message beats the messenger.** Like a great joke, contagious content spreads regardless of who tells it. This rebuts the "find the influential few" thesis: big forest fires aren't caused by big sparks — thousands of ordinary people carry the flame.
- **Word of mouth is mostly offline.** Only ~7% happens online (people guess ~50%); offline conversation is more frequent and often more persuasive. So the goal isn't "get social shares" — it's "get people talking," anywhere.

Word of mouth matters because it is *targeted* (naturally reaches interested people) and *credible* (friends tell it straight), making it ~10× more effective than advertising.

## Framework / model
**STEPPS** — six independent drivers of sharing. They are toppings, not a recipe: the more the better, but any subset works.

| **T**riggers | What cues make people think of it? | Top of mind, tip of tongue | [[Triggers - Top of Mind, Tip of Tongue]] |

## Application
- **Diagnose, then design.** Run any product, campaign, or idea through the six questions above; you rarely need all six, but you want at least one working hard.
- **Some drivers suit some jobs.** Nonprofits find Emotion easily; physical products find Public easily; almost anything has Practical Value. The wins often come from the *unlikely* driver — a blender is useful (Practical Value) but went viral on Social Currency.
- **Immediate vs. ongoing.** Interesting things get *immediate* buzz that fades; frequently *Triggered* things get sustained, ongoing buzz. Match the driver to whether you need a launch spike or a durable habit (see Triggers).
- **Build in either layer.** STEPPS can live in the product itself (the $100 cheesesteak *is* the Social Currency) or in the messaging around it (Kit Kat didn't change; its *coffee* pairing did).

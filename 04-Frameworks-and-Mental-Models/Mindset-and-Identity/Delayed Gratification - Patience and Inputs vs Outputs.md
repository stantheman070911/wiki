# Delayed Gratification - Patience and Inputs vs Outputs

## One-line summary
The poor delay pain; the ambitious delay gratification — and the way to endure the wait is to be patient with outputs and impatient with inputs.

## Context
Hormozi's meta-skill of wealth-building is the ability to act without an immediate reward. This entry captures how he operationalizes patience so it becomes action rather than waiting.

## Key insights
- **Delayed pain vs. delayed gratification.** The poor take pleasure now and pain later (diet Monday, invest "someday"), then act surprised when yesterday's deferred pain arrives as today. Flip it: take the small pain/risk now for the compounding gain later.
- **Patience is "figuring out what to do in the meantime."** It is not passive waiting; it is finding productive action while outcomes lag.
- **Patient with outputs, impatient with inputs.** Outputs (money, weight, followers) are outcomes you don't control; inputs (calls, reps, calories) are actions you do. When you feel "why hasn't this happened yet?", that's output-anxiety — answer it with more input. "Action alleviates anxiety."
- **Delayed gratification is a function of intelligence.** Across species, the ability to abstract a reward across a longer delay tracks trainability. Deliberately widening your reward window raises your rate of learning even as your reward rate falls short-term.
- **Perception of time accelerates with age**, so "patience" gets easier not because you change but because a year becomes a smaller fraction of your life — over-index on patience early.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Whenever the "should be happening faster" feeling hits, convert it directly into one more rep of the controllable input.
- Judge a day by inputs completed, not outputs received, so you can "win" daily regardless of lagging results.
- Aim to earn one outsized early feedback loop (first sale, first lead, first 100 followers); it disproportionately reinforces the behavior and lets obsession take over.

## Notable examples
- Hormozi's first podcast: ~4.5 years and 300–400 episodes at ~2–3k downloads/month before it took off — patience with the output, relentless with the input of publishing.
- Reframing "20 pounds heavier in four years" by future-pacing the specific pain, to make change less painful than staying the same (Tony Robbins' pain-of-change threshold).

# Paid on Results vs. Paid on Time

## One-line summary
Getting paid by the hour or on a steady salary buys security, but it also hard-caps income at your available hours; rich people accept the lack of guarantee that comes with being paid on results — commission, profit share, equity, or ownership — because that's the only structure with no ceiling.

## Context
Wealth File #11. Eker's core objection to the "go to school, get a steady job" script isn't the job itself, but the ceiling it silently imposes: "there's nothing wrong with getting a steady paycheck, unless it interferes with your ability to earn what you're worth. There's the rub. It usually does."

## Key insights
- **Security-based pay is fear-based pay.** Wanting a guaranteed steady amount is really saying "I'm afraid I won't earn enough on my own performance, so I'll settle for enough to survive or be comfortable" — the security has a real cost, and the cost is upside.
- **"Never have a ceiling on your income."** Trading time for money mechanically caps earnings because time is finite — this applies to salaried employees and to personal-service businesses alike (a massage therapist can't scale past their own hours; a pen distributor can fulfill an order of any size).
- **A concrete test of belief in your own value:** when a consultant wanted a flat $4,000/month fee, Eker offered 50% of the media value she generated instead (which her own numbers implied would pay her *more*). She declined — evidence that fear of a results-based structure, not the math, was the real blocker.
- **Ownership structures that convert time-pay into results-pay:** starting a business, working on commission or percentage of revenue, taking stock options/profit sharing, converting a job into a contract/consulting relationship, or joining network marketing — all trade guaranteed income for uncapped income.

## Tactics / how to apply
- If currently on salary or hourly wage, propose a compensation structure to your employer that ties at least part of your pay to individual or company results.
- If you run a business, restructure supplier or employee compensation to include a results-based component rather than pure time-based pay.
- If a results-based structure isn't available where you are, evaluate starting a side business, joining as a commissioned salesperson, or converting to an independent contractor relationship — the tax and leverage benefits of business ownership compound the income benefit.

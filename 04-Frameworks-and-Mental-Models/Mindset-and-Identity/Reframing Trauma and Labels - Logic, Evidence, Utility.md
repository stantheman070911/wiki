# Reframing Trauma and Labels - Logic, Evidence, Utility

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Frameworks and Mental Models Index|Frameworks and Mental Models]] → [[04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Mindset-and-Identity/Mindset and Identity Map|Mindset and Identity]]

## One-line summary
"Demons," "trauma," and "I'm-the-type-of-person-who" labels are just behaviors under conditions; define them concretely, test them, and you regain the ability to change.

## Context
Hormozi argues that mystical self-talk ("I have demons," "it's stored in my body," "I'm not a math person") is a convenient story that hands your power to an unchangeable past. Reduce it to observable behavior and it becomes solvable.

## Key insights
- **Trauma is just "a permanent change in behavior from an aversive stimulus"** — and can be good (touching a hot stove once). What matters is only whether the resulting behavior serves your goals.
- **Identity is a lie; doing is being.** We describe who people are by what they do (John the Carpenter). Because you control your actions, your identity is 100% under your control — the labels change your behavior far more than your behavior changes the labels.
- **"Because" statements are traps.** "I can't get close to people *because* my mom…" hands a present, changeable behavior to a fixed, unchangeable past. Drop the because; keep only "I have behaved this way, period," then reward the new behavior in the present.
- **The one razor.** For any belief, person, activity, or possession, ask: *does this make it more or less likely that I hit my goal?* Nothing else needs adjudicating.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
**Logic → Evidence → Utility (Dr. Cashy).**
1. **Logic** — "What does that mean?" Translate the vague claim into a specific behavior ("I self-sabotage" → "after ~2 months on time, I start showing up late").
2. **Evidence** — "How do you know?" Look for actual data. Usually it happened a handful of times, or the person has none — i.e., it's a narrative, not a fact.
3. **Utility** — "Who cares? In relation to what?" Judge it only against your goals; if it blocks a goal, change the behavior; if not, drop it.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Rewrite any "I am / I always / I can't" statement as a neutral description of past behavior, then design a present reward for the behavior you want instead.
- Use the court-evidence test: "John said these words" is evidence; "I felt offended" or "I think he meant" is not. Ignore intentions and stories; act on facts.
- Deliberately break your own labels (Hormozi went from "bad at math until 25" to doing mental math daily by simply deciding to and dropping the calculator).

## Notable examples
- A young man insisting he had "demons"; unpacked, it meant "under condition X, I behave Y" — a skill deficiency, not a spiritual one.
- Hormozi has to actively defend against hometown labels ("hothead," "short temper") — a case for changing your *environment* so it stops reinforcing labels you then perpetuate.

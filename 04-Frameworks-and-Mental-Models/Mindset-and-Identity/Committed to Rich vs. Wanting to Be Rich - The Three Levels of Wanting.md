# Committed to Rich vs. Wanting to Be Rich - The Three Levels of Wanting

## One-line summary
Most people who say they want to be rich are sending the universe (and themselves) mixed messages, because a hidden file of "reasons wealth would be bad" is running alongside the stated desire — true wealth requires resolving that conflict into unconditional commitment.

## Context
Wealth File #3. Eker's seminar exercise — asking people to list the possible downsides of getting rich (losing it and looking like a failure, not knowing if people like you for you, tax burden, kidnapping risk, the hassle of managing money) — surfaces a mixed internal message that quietly sabotages wealth-seeking.

## Key insights
- **Three levels of wanting, in ascending strength:** "I want to be rich" (I'll take it if it falls in my lap — wanting alone never converts to having, and creates a loop of more wanting); "I choose to be rich" (stronger, ties to responsibility for outcomes); "I commit to being rich" (devoting yourself unreservedly — "I will be rich or I will die trying").
- **The number one reason people don't get what they want is that they don't know what they want** — mixed internal messages ("money is fun" vs. "rich people are greedy") cancel each other out.
- **Commitment means no conditions on effort, time, or sacrifice.** Most people who claim commitment actually have unstated limits on how much they're willing to risk or give up — that's the tell that it's still just "trying," not commitment.
- **W.H. Murray's commitment principle:** the moment you definitively commit, unforeseen support ("providence") starts moving in your favor — but only after the commitment, not before.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Write a short, specific paragraph on exactly why creating wealth matters to you — vague reasons signal mixed internal messages.
- Make the commitment external and social: state it aloud to a supportive person with a hand on your heart ("I hereby commit to becoming a millionaire or more by [date]") and have them respond "I believe in you."
- Notice your own reaction to committing: freedom or a tinge of fear both indicate you're on the right track; indifference or avoidance signals you're still in "wanting" mode.

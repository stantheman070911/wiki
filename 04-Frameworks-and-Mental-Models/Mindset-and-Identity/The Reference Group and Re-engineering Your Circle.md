# The Reference Group and Re-engineering Your Circle

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Frameworks and Mental Models Index|Frameworks and Mental Models]] → [[04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Mindset-and-Identity/Mindset and Identity Map|Mindset and Identity]]

## One-line summary
Your future is set by the ~5 people you compare yourself to (not the ones you spend the most time with), so listen to those closest to your goals, cut the friends who lower your odds, and accept a lonely in-between season.

## Context
Hormozi cites the Harvard finding on "reference group" and sharpens it: it's whose voice you hear when making a big decision, not who you hang out with. Re-engineering that group is the fastest way to change your life.

## Key insights
- **Reference group ≠ time spent.** It's the ~5 people you *compare yourself to* and whose voices you hear at decision points. As you evolve it can become imaginary (heroes you transpose decisions onto). "You don't become like the people you admire; you become like the people you want to impress."
- **Listen to those closest to your goals, not closest to you.** Family is more aligned than strangers but not fully aligned — only future-you is. Parents typically play "not to lose"; to play to win you take different bets.
- **A friend is someone who decreases your probability of failure.** For each person ask: does this person make it more or less likely I hit my goal? It's binary. Three friends who each cut your odds 30% leave you far below where you started.
- **Cut friends who only talk about your past.** If they can only reminisce, their best years are behind them; keep the ones who talk about the future.
- **The lonely in-between.** You'll usually go from a group of friends → no friends → one or two at a new level, repeatedly. "To be exceptional is to be the exception" — it's normal for normal people to find you abnormal. Loneliness is where the disproportionate work gets done.
- **Standards are borrowed.** If you don't know successful people, borrow a measuring stick: "Is this the standard Musk/Bezos/Jobs would accept?" You rose only because "what they deemed acceptable, I deemed intolerable."
- **Winners focus on winning; losers focus on winners.** Hate comes from below, never above.
- **Environment changes the normal action.** Repeated exposure to ambitious builders, mentors, accelerators, and unfamiliar high-standard rooms makes behaviors that once felt awkward become ordinary; information inputs are part of that environment too.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
**Five questions to decide whether to keep a friend:**
1. If told you're a lot like this friend, would you take it as a compliment?
2. Are you truly fulfilled by the friendship, or just avoiding loneliness?
3. Can you be unapologetically yourself around them?
4. Do you like who they are *today*, or the idea of who they could be?
5. Would you want your future child to be friends with someone like this?

Plus a partner/friend filter: same *scale* of goals (not identical goals) and the same level of dedication to them.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Audit your circle with the five questions and the "more/less likely" test; expect to shed friendships each season and let past friends be "right for that season."
- Change your environment to stop it reinforcing old labels and low standards.
- When weighing a big decision, notice whose voice appears — that's your real reference group; if they don't have what you want, disregard their advice.

## Notable examples
- Hormozi talks to one friend from school and one or two from college; convenience-based friendships fade as conditions change.
- He used the goal-scale + dedication filter to choose Leila as his most important reference point.

# The Maker Mix - Money, Impact, and Expression

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Frameworks and Mental Models Index|Frameworks and Mental Models]] → [[04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Life-Leadership-and-Wellbeing/Life Leadership and Wellbeing Map|Life, Leadership, and Wellbeing]]

## One-line summary
Work does not have to choose between money and meaning; it can be designed as a changing mix of moneymaking, impact-making, and expression-making.

## Context
People become dissatisfied when they measure one kind of contribution with another kind's yardstick—for example, judging art only by sales or mission work by a software salary. The Maker Mix makes the intended rewards explicit and allows them to be distributed across work and life.

## Key insights
- **Money versus meaning is a false dichotomy.** Money, impact, and expression are independent dimensions rather than mutually exclusive careers.
- **Intent determines the measure.** Paid creative work can produce all three, but the reason you do an activity clarifies which return matters most.
- **No universal mix is optimal.** A high-money season to repay debt can be coherent, as can a low-money season devoted to service or art.
- **A job need not satisfy the whole mix.** Income can come from one role while community, service, or creative practice supplies the others.
- **Misapplied measures create avoidable pain.** An artist may value expression while demanding market validation; a nonprofit leader may create enormous impact while judging success by private-sector pay.
- **The mix changes over a lifetime.** Conscious adjustment is the aim, not permanent balance.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
Create two three-slider boards, each running from 0 to 100:

1. **Current mix:** set Money, Impact, and Expression according to how large and important each feels—not merely hours spent.
2. **Desired next mix:** adjust each slider independently to represent one manageable step toward greater coherency.
3. Compare the boards and choose a prototype that moves only one slider.

Use an Impact Map alongside the mix when needed: locate a role by its type of impact (repair, sustain, or create new) and its point of impact (personal through global).

## Tactics / how to apply
- Write what success means in each dimension before moving any sliders.
- Audit work, family, creative, and community roles together; the mix belongs to the whole life.
- Identify where a market metric has displaced the metric you actually value.
- Prototype an adjustment with a small paid project, volunteer role, class, or protected studio time.
- Revisit the board during life transitions and whenever achievement feels strangely unsatisfying.

## Notable examples
Bill Burnett accepted a large pay cut to teach at Stanford because the move raised his impact while keeping enough money. He later protected weekends in an art studio to begin shifting expression upward before making it his primary activity.

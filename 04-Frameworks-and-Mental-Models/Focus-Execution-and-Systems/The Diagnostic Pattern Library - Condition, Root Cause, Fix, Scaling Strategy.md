# The Diagnostic Pattern Library - Condition, Root Cause, Fix, Scaling Strategy

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Frameworks and Mental Models Index|Frameworks and Mental Models]]

## One-line summary
The recurring "if you observe A, it's usually caused by B; the immediate fix is C; the long-term scaling play is D" patterns that show up again and again when Hormozi diagnoses businesses — a lookup table you can run against your own.

## Context
Across a long run of live teardowns (railings, styling, garbage collection, travel courses, lash salons, restaurants, HVAC, chiropractic chains, music-law, couples-coaching), the same symptoms keep resolving to the same root causes and the same fixes. This entry generalizes those into reusable patterns. Use it alongside the sequence in [[04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Focus-Execution-and-Systems/The Business Teardown - Hormozi's Diagnostic Method|The Business Teardown]] — that entry is *how to look*; this one is *what you'll find*.

## The patterns

**1. Nonsensical unit economics (LTV:CAC of 1:1, 41:1, "the numbers don't make sense").**
- Root cause: broken **attribution / data** — "we have numbers but they're not useful."
- Fix: instrument attribution first; if needed, briefly turn ads *off* to see what sales actually depend on them. Don't optimize on unvalidated data.
- Scaling: only after data is trustworthy, move budget from losing campaigns to winners and scale spend with confidence.

**2. Lots of clicks, almost no sales (e.g. 1,800 clicks → 8 sales).**
- Root cause: **nothing to opt into** — no lead magnet, asking for the high-commitment action (buy/book) too early, so you only skim the tiny "in-pain-right-now" sliver.
- Fix: add a genuinely valuable, **risk-free/easy/fast** lead magnet gated to *qualified* prospects, a short VSL before the call, and BAND qualification (Budget, Authority, Need, Timing).
- Scaling: a funnel that converts colder traffic lets you raise spend into much larger audiences (Schwartz's [[01-Business-Strategy/Traffic-and-Acquisition/The Buyer Awareness Pyramid and the Halo Strategy|awareness levels]]).

**3. "I've run out of time / we're maxed out" — with healthy margins.**
- Root cause: **supply constraint**, often self-inflicted by over-customizing or giving clients unlimited personal access (e.g. 24/7 Slack).
- Fix: pare the offer (trim & stack) to deliver the same *perceived* value with far less of your time; cut the least-loved, most-time-hungry components.
- Scaling: productize/templatize so capacity multiplies 5-10×; only then add headcount. (If margins are *thin* instead, the root cause is a mispriced offer — see pattern 8.)

**4. Low repeat purchase / weak back end.**
- Root cause: either the product isn't naturally recurring, or value isn't being demonstrated (people leave after ~2 payments = you're not showing results).
- Fix: don't force continuity where it doesn't fit; instead **long-term nurture** (be first in mind when they re-buy) and **BAM — book-a-meeting-from-a-meeting** (rebook while they're in front of you; credited to Sharran Srivatsaa). If there *is* a back end, log into their data and show week-over-week wins so value is visible.
- Scaling: fix churn before spending on traffic — retention is the foundation that lets growth stack.

**5. Price war / commodity auction (lowest bid wins, margins bleeding).**
- Root cause: serving **price-sensitive, low-differentiation buyers**; you're really competing in the *efficiency* business.
- Fix: shift toward the less price-sensitive, higher-margin segment you already have the most of (e.g. DIY/custom over contractors); stop chasing the commoditized buyer.
- Scaling: if the commodity segment is structurally unavoidable, make operational **efficiency** the moat, because it's the only durable way to win an auction.

**6. Negative margin / capital-intensive / chronic cash crunch.**
- Root cause: **cash collected in the first 30 days < fully-loaded COGS + CAC**, so growth burns cash.
- Fix: construct the offer so upfront cash clears that bar — prepay terms (12/6/3-month AB anchor), reframed fees (a "bin fee" that actually covers first-period labor), financing, small add-on upsells. Target a blended average cash-per-sale above the break-even line.
- Scaling: grow debt-free because each new customer self-funds; then reinvest. (See [[01-Business-Strategy/Pricing-and-Offers/The Money Model - Sequencing Offers to Fund Growth|The Money Model]] and [[01-Business-Strategy/Traffic-and-Acquisition/Client-Financed Acquisition - Make the Customer Fund the Next Customer|Client-Financed Acquisition]].)

**7. Single-channel dependence (e.g. 81% from one source).**
- Root cause: fragility — one algorithm/platform change can sink the business.
- Fix: don't diversify first; **max the working channel** (more, then better) since it's proven and profitable.
- Scaling: add a second channel only once the first is optimized — a second working channel more than doubles enterprise value by removing the single point of failure.

**8. Spread thin / stalled (multiple avatars, many channels, several products).**
- Root cause: **no focus** — trying to be everything.
- Fix: collapse to **one avatar, one channel, one product** (to ~$1M); if margins are thin, the offer is usually mispriced (undercharging because you "sell out of your own wallet").
- Scaling: expand only after the single model is nailed; "better creates bigger, bigger creates bloat" — get better first and bigger follows.

**9. Under-monetized traffic / assets sitting idle (big audience, small revenue).**
- Root cause: traffic routed to low-yield destinations, or a high-leverage asset used once and abandoned.
- Fix: point *all* eyeballs through the single most-efficient monetization vehicle you already have (e.g. every YouTube CTA → the DM channel that actually closes); reactivate the asset (6 affiliates from 40 emails → send 400 emails; an email list marketed to 2×/week).
- Scaling: 10×-ing a channel that's already a third of revenue more than triples the business — highest leverage, lowest cost.

**10. Temporal supply/demand imbalance (slammed weekends, dead weekdays).**
- Root cause: flat pricing against a variable demand curve.
- Fix: **surge-price the peak** (a separate weekend menu at +$2/item, peak-day pricing), framed as a weekday discount.
- Scaling: smooths the demand curve, lifts staff utilization, and raises availability at peak — more revenue from the same fixed cost.

## Application
- Match your symptom to a pattern, then work its four cells in order: confirm the root cause (don't fix the symptom), apply the immediate fix, then set the long-term scaling play.
- When several patterns fire at once, sequence them: data/attribution and leaks before spend; fast-and-light before heavy (see [[04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Focus-Execution-and-Systems/More, Better, New - The Scaling Priority Sequence|More, Better, New]]).
- Re-diagnose after each fix — relieving one constraint exposes the next.

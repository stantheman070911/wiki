# The 5A Operating System - Alignment, Awareness, Accountabilities, Activities, and Assets

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Frameworks and Mental Models Index|Frameworks and Mental Models]] → [[04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Focus-Execution-and-Systems/Focus Execution and Systems Map|Focus, Execution, and Systems]]

## One-line summary
Turn a desired future into a ninety-day operating cycle by aligning people to the end state, surfacing uncomfortable realities, assigning outcomes, repeating the right activities, and investing in assets that make later cycles easier.

## Context
Goals fail when they remain personal wishes detached from the people, constraints, work, and capital required to produce them. The 5A system connects aspiration to a recurring team cadence.

## Key insights
- **Alignment creates pull.** The end state must be vivid enough to reveal the roadmap, rewards, sacrifices, and people whose cooperation is required.
- **Awareness prevents polite failure.** Concerns, blind spots, weak economics, and missing capabilities should be logged and discussed before they become crises.
- **Accountabilities attach problems to owners.** A priority without a capable “who,” authority, resources, and a deadline is still an observation.
- **Activities turn the quarter into weeks.** Each person needs a small set of recurring, high-value commitments that can be checked objectively.
- **Assets create compounding.** Buying, developing, or improving assets should reduce future effort, increase reach, or improve reliability rather than merely add activity.
- **Alignment decays.** Teams should expect to reenroll around the vision and reward structure every quarter.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
Run the sequence every ninety days:

1. **Alignment:** define the three-year end state, roadmap, reward structure, and people who must commit.
2. **Awareness:** maintain a shared list of facts, doubts, constraints, and emerging risks.
3. **Accountabilities:** select the three to six breakthroughs for the quarter and assign one owner to each.
4. **Activities:** translate breakthroughs into three to six weekly commitments per person, declared Monday and reviewed Friday.
5. **Assets:** choose the few investments—bought, developed, or improved—that make the operating system stronger next quarter.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Create two living documents: an alignment narrative and an awareness list.
- Normalize the phrase “Can I raise an awareness?” so junior people can surface bad news without political risk.
- At the quarterly reset, resolve or assign every material awareness; do not let the list become a graveyard.
- Use Monday peer challenge to improve each person's weekly priorities and Friday done/not-done reporting to preserve accountability.
- Compare potential assets on expected leverage, not purchase price or prestige.
- Include spouses, co-founders, and other materially affected people in alignment when their unspoken resistance could stop the plan.

## Notable examples
Priestley contrasts a costly property producing modest rent with a much smaller investment in a lead-generation asset capable of producing recurring demand. Asset selection should be based on the bottleneck and return, not on conventional status.

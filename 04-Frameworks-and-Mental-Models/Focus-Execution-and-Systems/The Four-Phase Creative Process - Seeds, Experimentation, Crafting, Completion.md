# The Four-Phase Creative Process - Seeds, Experimentation, Crafting, Completion

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Frameworks and Mental Models Index|Frameworks and Mental Models]] → [[04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Focus-Execution-and-Systems/Focus Execution and Systems Map|Focus, Execution, and Systems]]

## One-line summary
Strong creative work moves through four different operating modes—collect without judging, explore without pruning, build with discipline, then refine and release—and each mode fails when governed by the rules of another.

## Context
Creative blocks often come from performing the right activity at the wrong time: evaluating a seed before it can grow, continuing to explore after a direction is clear, or polishing work that has not found its form.

## Key insights
- **Seeds are possibilities, not commitments.** Capture many starting points before assigning value; initial intensity does not reliably predict eventual quality.
- **Experimentation generates information.** Develop seeds in several directions and follow the versions that produce energy, surprise, or curiosity. Premature editing removes paths before their value can be known.
- **Craft turns discovery into form.** Once a direction has revealed itself, narrow the problem and do the less glamorous work of construction, addition, subtraction, and skillful execution.
- **Completion changes the governing question.** Stop asking what else the work could become and ask whether its present form expresses its essence at the highest available standard.
- **The phases are directional, not rigid.** A discovery during Craft may return the work to Experimentation; a final review may expose a structural gap. Move backward deliberately, then advance again.
- **Emotional energy is a signal, not a verdict.** Excitement helps choose among live possibilities, but every worthwhile project also contains neutral or laborious stretches.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
1. **Seed — receive:** collect broadly; do not rank too early.
2. **Experiment — expand:** generate variants, questions, and collisions; delay pruning.
3. **Craft — converge:** choose a living direction, build it fully, and remove what weakens the core.
4. **Complete — release:** inspect the whole, correct what matters, declare the iteration finished, and send it into the world.

At every transition, explicitly name the current phase. Then use its matching criterion: **interest → vitality → coherence → readiness**.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Keep a seed bank and review it at intervals; distance makes relative energy easier to see.
- In Experimentation, create physical or observable versions instead of debating imagined versions.
- When Craft stalls on one section, complete everything around it; context often reveals the missing bridge.
- Work on more than one project when distance would improve judgment, but focus on one when its charge is unusually strong.
- Use provisional deadlines during Craft for momentum and firmer deadlines during Completion to force release.
- At phase changes, preserve earlier versions so exploration can resume without fear of losing what already works.

## Notable examples
Chinese alchemists seeking immortality produced gunpowder; penicillin and Post-it notes likewise emerged as unplanned results. The reusable lesson is not that accidents guarantee breakthroughs, but that an experiment must be observed for what it produced rather than judged only against its original intention.

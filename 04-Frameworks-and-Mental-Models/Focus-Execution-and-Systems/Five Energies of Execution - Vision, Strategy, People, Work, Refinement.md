# Five Energies of Execution - Vision, Strategy, People, Work, Refinement

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Frameworks and Mental Models Index|Frameworks and Mental Models]] → [[04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Focus-Execution-and-Systems/Focus Execution and Systems Map|Focus, Execution, and Systems]]

## One-line summary
Progress stalls when a project keeps applying one mode of effort to every problem; execution requires shifting deliberately among vision, strategy, people, work, and refinement.

## Context
Optimism alone is not execution, and relentless activity is not always the missing ingredient. A project moves when its current constraint is matched with the right kind of energy.

## Key insights
- **Purpose and vision create direction.** This mode defines the future worth pursuing and gives the team a reason to care.
- **Strategy converts desire into choices.** It selects a path, sequences moves, and decides what will not be done.
- **People create alignment and leverage.** Recruiting, storytelling, incentives, and shared ownership turn an individual ambition into coordinated capacity.
- **Work produces the result.** Some constraints yield only to calls made, products shipped, customers served, and repeated physical execution.
- **Refinement compounds learning.** Data, feedback, systems, and iteration make the next cycle cheaper and more reliable.
- **A mode can become a hiding place.** Vision without work becomes fantasy; work without strategy becomes waste; refinement without shipping becomes perfectionism.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
Run a five-question constraint scan:

1. **Vision:** Are we clear and emotionally committed to the outcome?
2. **Strategy:** Do we have a credible route and explicit priorities?
3. **People:** Are the right people enrolled, aligned, and rewarded?
4. **Work:** Are we completing the necessary volume of action?
5. **Refinement:** Are feedback and results changing how the system operates?

The weakest answer identifies the energy to emphasize next. Recheck after the constraint moves rather than turning one energy into a permanent identity.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Label meeting agendas by the energy required: envision, decide, align, execute, or improve.
- When a project feels stuck, ask which mode has been overused and which has been avoided.
- Give every vision session an execution owner and every execution sprint a review date.
- Separate creation from refinement so feedback improves released work instead of delaying it indefinitely.
- Treat personal information environments as energy inputs; remove channels that reliably reduce useful action.

## Notable examples
Priestley describes leaving a high-reach social platform after its outrage-heavy feed lowered his energy. The audience metric looked positive, but the downstream effect on judgment and action was negative.

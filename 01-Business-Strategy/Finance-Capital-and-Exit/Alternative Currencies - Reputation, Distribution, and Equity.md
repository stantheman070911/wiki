# Alternative Currencies - Reputation, Distribution, and Equity

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[01-Business-Strategy/Business Strategy Index|Business Strategy]]

## One-line summary
Beyond cash and time, a business can accumulate reputation, audience access, intellectual property, relationships, and priced equity that can be exchanged for talent, partnerships, acquisitions, and future opportunity.

## Context
People who recognize only wages and cash tend to trade hours for money. Owners can build assets whose exchange value exceeds the effort required for any single transaction, but those currencies work only when counterparties trust their quality and terms.

## Key insights
- **Reputation reduces perceived risk.** A credible person can lend attention, confidence, or governance to a venture and receive access or ownership in return.
- **Distribution creates launch power.** An engaged email list, audience, retail channel, or customer base can make an offer oversubscribed and therefore becomes negotiable value.
- **Priced equity becomes acquisition currency.** An external investment establishes an implied company value, making a share exchange easier to discuss with talent or acquisition targets.
- **IP and media preserve value outside the room.** Methods, brands, content, data, and software can keep creating utility after the creator's time has moved elsewhere.
- **A currency needs liquidity and credibility.** Inflated follower counts, unproven valuations, or vague adviser titles cannot reliably purchase real assets.
- **Non-cash exchange can hide cost.** Equity is permanent participation in future upside; use it only when the acquired value and alignment justify the dilution.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
Inventory five currencies:

1. **Reputation:** trusted association and demonstrated judgment.
2. **Distribution:** permissioned access to relevant demand.
3. **IP and data:** reusable knowledge, evidence, and proprietary context.
4. **Equity:** ownership with a credible valuation and legal structure.
5. **Relationships:** the ability to assemble talent, capital, and opportunities.

For each, define how it is measured, who values it, what it can purchase, and the long-term cost of exchanging it.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Track audience quality, engagement, and conversion instead of treating follower count as currency by itself.
- Document adviser, endorsement, and distribution agreements so both sides understand what is being exchanged.
- Build reusable IP before trying to license or trade it.
- Obtain independent legal and financial advice before using equity for compensation or acquisitions.
- Compare the future value surrendered with the speed, capability, or asset acquired.
- Protect reputation by declining exchanges that monetize trust faster than value is delivered.

## Notable examples
Priestley describes a company that sells a minority stake to establish an external valuation, then uses another portion of its shares in a proposed acquisition. The shares function like currency because another party has already priced and trusted them.

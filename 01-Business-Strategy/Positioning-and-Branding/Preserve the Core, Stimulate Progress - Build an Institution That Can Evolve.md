# Preserve the Core, Stimulate Progress - Build an Institution That Can Evolve

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[01-Business-Strategy/Business Strategy Index|Business Strategy]]

## One-line summary
Enduring organizations hold their core values and purpose fixed while continually changing strategies, practices, products, and ambitious goals.

## Context
Sustained performance is not the same as institutional durability. A company becomes capable of lasting across leaders and product cycles when it knows what must never change and gives everything else permission—even pressure—to evolve.

## Key insights
- **Core ideology has two parts.** Core values are enduring principles; core purpose is the reason the organization exists beyond making money.
- **Profit is necessary but not sufficient.** Like blood in a body, it sustains the institution without being the institution's reason for living.
- **The content of values is less important than authenticity and consistency.** Values create durability only when they actually govern selection, tradeoffs, and behavior.
- **Preservation and change are complementary.** The organization protects its identity by adapting its manifestations to new conditions.
- **Clock building beats time telling.** The aim is a system that can produce good decisions and renew itself without depending on one leader or idea.
- **A good BHAG comes from understanding.** An ambitious goal should sit inside the three circles; a dramatic goal outside them is bravado, not strategy.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
**Preserve:**

- Core values — principles the organization would keep even if they became temporarily costly.
- Core purpose — the contribution that makes the organization's existence meaningful beyond profit.

**Stimulate progress:**

- Change operating practices, structures, strategies, technologies, products, and markets as reality changes.
- Set a clear, compelling BHAG only where capability, economics, and passion intersect.

The governing question is: *Is this element core ideology, or merely a current expression of it?*

## Tactics / how to apply
- List claimed values and test each with a costly hypothetical; remove any principle the organization would abandon when inconvenient.
- Express purpose as an enduring contribution, not a product category, growth target, or financial result.
- Separate immutable principles from revisable practices in policies and strategy documents.
- When retiring a product or process, explain how the change protects or advances the core rather than framing continuity as sameness.
- Test every major goal against the Hedgehog Concept before using it to mobilize the organization.
- Make succession a design requirement: preserve values through people selection while allowing the next generation to improve the system.

## Notable examples
Hewlett-Packard preserved technical contribution, respect, and purpose while moving through generations of products. Disney kept creative imagination, attention to detail, and the aim of bringing happiness while expanding from cartoons into films, television, parks, and other formats. Boeing's commercial-jet BHAG worked because large-aircraft capability, attractive economics, and genuine passion had converged.

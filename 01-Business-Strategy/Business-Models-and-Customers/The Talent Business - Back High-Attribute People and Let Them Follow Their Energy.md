# The Talent Business - Back High-Attribute People and Let Them Follow Their Energy

## One-line summary
The most powerful business formula is a high-attribute person doing something that genuinely lights them up over a long period — so the highest-leverage move is to back such people and give them permission to follow their own energy.

## Context
Graham Weaver ran Alpine Investors as a private-equity firm. Working on himself, he realized he no longer had energy for PE itself — but noticed that his best deals were the ones where they had backed a high-attribute person, dropped them into running a company, and effectively gave them permission to follow their own second voice and find the work that lit them up. Those weren't just the most fulfilling deals; **they were also the highest-performing ones.** Alpine shifted from being in the private-equity business to being in the *talent business*. From that pivot (2014 onward), the firm's assets grew over 50×.

## The insight
- **The formula:** *high-attribute person × work that lights them up × a long time horizon.* All three factors are required; it's the most powerful combination Weaver has encountered for producing extraordinary results.
- **Fulfillment and performance are correlated, not traded off.** Backing people to do energizing work produced the best returns *and* the best experience — not a soft compromise on results.
- **You can't fake your way to extraordinary work.** You can push through work you don't care about for a while, but you burn out; extraordinary work "requires every part of you over a really long period of time" — i.e., it requires tapping the person's [[Trust Your Second Voice - Energy Is the Language of Your Soul|second voice]], not just their compliance.
- **The leader must go first.** The unlock wasn't only backing others — it was Weaver letting go of his own fears and following his own energy, then giving his team permission to do the same. A leader running on fear can't grant others the freedom he won't take himself (see [[Fire the Coach - Your Inner Critic Is Just Fear]] on how a leader's inner state propagates to the team).

## Application
- **Reframe what business you're in.** Ask whether your real leverage is the asset/process you *think* you sell, or the caliber of people you back. If talent is the multiplier, organize the company around finding and unleashing it.
- **Hire for attributes, then assign for energy.** Select high-attribute people; then match them to the work that visibly lights them up rather than only to the role you need filled. Use the energy test — where does this person come alive? — as a real staffing input.
- **Grant permission explicitly.** Give operators genuine ownership and the latitude to move toward what energizes them, over a long horizon; don't manage them from your own fear.
- **Model it.** Do your own internal work first; a team led by someone following their second voice is freed to follow theirs.

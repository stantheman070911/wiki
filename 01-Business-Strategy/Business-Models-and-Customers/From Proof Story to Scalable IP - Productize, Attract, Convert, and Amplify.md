# From Proof Story to Scalable IP - Productize, Attract, Convert, and Amplify

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[01-Business-Strategy/Business Strategy Index|Business Strategy]] → [[01-Business-Strategy/Business-Models-and-Customers/Business Models and Customers Map|Business Models and Customers]]

## One-line summary
Start with a result you can prove, extract the repeatable method as intellectual property, package a low-risk learning product and a core offer, then scale only after the attention-to-sale economics are visible.

## Context
Expertise is difficult to commercialize when it remains an unstructured personal story. A proof story turns lived experience into a named outcome and method that prospects can understand, test, and buy.

## Key insights
- **Proof precedes positioning.** A credible foundation is a recent situation where you did something special for a recognizable customer type, produced a measurable result, named it, and can explain the steps.
- **IP is structured experience.** The story, data, principles, examples, language, and sequence become more valuable when organized into a transferable method.
- **Learning and delivery are separate products.** A product for prospects teaches why the method matters; the core offer applies or delivers it.
- **Attention has three thresholds.** People must notice you through repeated short-form exposure, know you through substantial long-form time, and rate you through credible associations.
- **Signals create the sales pipeline.** A landing page converts attention into an explicit expression of interest with a hook, value proposition, credibility, and call to action.
- **Scale comes last.** Paid promotion is rational only after lead-to-sale conversion reveals an allowable cost per lead and sale.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
Use the five-step chain:

1. **Proof-backed product:** extract the proof story and package IP into a product for prospects plus core offer.
2. **Attention:** build repeated short-form visibility, several hours of long-form trust, and relevant associations.
3. **Signal:** collect explicit interest through a focused landing page.
4. **Conversion:** manage leads, appointments, presentations, and sales.
5. **Amplification:** calculate allowable acquisition costs and gradually expand ads, outreach, partnerships, and sponsorships.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Review the last five years for several proof stories and look for a common customer and outcome.
- Name the outcome in customer language and document the method as steps, principles, and examples.
- Use an assessment, workshop, report, or book as the product for prospects; use a visual brochure or deck for the core offer.
- Make the landing page answer four questions: Why care? What value? Why trust you? What should I do next?
- Track every conversion in a LAPS dashboard before buying more traffic.
- Increase paid promotion gradually while actual cost per lead and sale remain below the allowable thresholds.

## Notable examples
Priestley traces his “Key Person of Influence” business to a proof story: helping credible experts become known through a repeatable five-part method, then turning that result into a book and accelerator.

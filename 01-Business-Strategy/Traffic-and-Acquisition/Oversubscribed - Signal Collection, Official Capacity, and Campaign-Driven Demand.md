# Oversubscribed - Signal Collection, Official Capacity, and Campaign-Driven Demand

## One-line summary
Create demand before opening sales by collecting more qualified signals than the business can serve, publishing a credible capacity constraint, and launching when logic, emotion, and urgency align.

## Context
Profit depends less on deserving a margin than on maintaining demand–supply tension. When capacity is freely available, customers can delay, compare, and negotiate; when demand visibly exceeds a real limit, the business can choose customers and protect price.

## Key insights
- **Demand–supply tension governs margin.** Excess demand tolerates profit, excess supply tolerates loss, and balance tends to tolerate only ordinary wages.
- **Only “your people” matter.** A small audience that notices, knows, and rates the business can be more valuable than broad, low-trust reach.
- **Market-making precedes sales-making.** Waiting lists, applications, registrations, and assessments reveal demand before inventory or service capacity is released.
- **Official capacity makes scarcity legible.** A constraint becomes commercially powerful when the number is real, public, and visibly smaller than qualified interest.
- **Buying conditions combine logic, emotion, and urgency.** Customers need a rational case, a desirable future they can feel, and a credible cost of waiting.
- **Remarkability replenishes demand.** An exceptional customer experience turns each fulfilled order into stories, referrals, and future signals.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
Run a signal-led launch:

1. Warm a defined audience through repeated short-form exposure, substantial long-form value, and credible presence across several contexts.
2. Open a signal-collection mechanism without yet selling the scarce offer.
3. Publish the official capacity and, where appropriate, the level of qualified demand.
4. Qualify signals and prepare the logic, emotional outcome, and real deadline.
5. Open sales at a defined moment, select the best-fit customers, and close when capacity is allocated.
6. Deliver a positively remarkable experience that increases the next campaign's signal pool.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Aim for several qualified signals per available place before launching.
- Use selection criteria that improve customer fit rather than artificial hoops designed only to create theater.
- State working rules, response boundaries, and availability early; a constrained business should not pretend to serve everyone.
- Give away insight and education while charging for implementation, access, and transformation.
- Operate three campaign horizons: weekly baseline demand, quarterly spotlights, and an annual message that compounds recognition.
- Track demand-to-capacity ratio, qualification rate, conversion, price integrity, referrals, and customer experience.

## Notable examples
Priestley uses Glastonbury Festival as the model: interest is collected long before a brief sales window, and disclosed demand greatly exceeds the available tickets. The sales event works because the market was made first.

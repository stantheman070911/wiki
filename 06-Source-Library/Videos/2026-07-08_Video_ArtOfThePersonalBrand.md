## Net-new signal (the reason to process this)
1. **Personal brand is permanent; the "views are down" flattening theorem; the 3–10k-view sweet spot.** Small niche brands win because algorithms fragment the market.
2. **Four levels of a personal brand (0–3)** and the **personal-brand vs creator** distinction.
3. **Five Level-1 archetypes:** carousel dumper, documentarian, hobbyist, casual poster, journeyman.
4. **"There's no competition" attrition model:** 100 → (80 never start) → (10 quit) → (5 shiny object) → (2 unlucky) → 3 left.
5. **Content-market fit = niche-maxing × content TAM;** three goals (get hired/paid, distribution, attract a partner).
6. **Positioning trio:** counter-position, mirror-with-a-twist, standout demographic.
7. **View-count troubleshooting ladder:** 0–500 (basics/"why care") → 1k (structure/hook) → 10–50k (repeat outliers) → 100k+ (systems/team). Plus don't-oversaturate, remix-by-format, attention hacking, tribal affinity.
8. **Timing milestone ladder:** 30 days (belief) → 100 (habit) → 365 (first money) → 2yr (plan) → 3yr (stay on plan) → 5yr (superpower).
9. **Creator monetization ladder:** contact-in-bio → email list → services → low-ticket → high-ticket DFY → brand deals → products; "why they watch is why they buy"; marquee property, signature series, go-wide.
10. **Content-concept formulas:** free work, niche-niche playbook, idea content, would/wouldn't expertise, curation.

## Caveats
- **Selection bias in the "no competition" model** — it's an encouraging heuristic, not a measured funnel; niches with tens of thousands of aspirants have a larger surviving pool (the creator says so).
- **The 3–10k-view "sweet spot"** is goal-dependent (career leverage), not a universal target — creators chasing scale need more.
- **HubSpot "Loop Marketing"** is sponsored content; treat as an ad, not an endorsement-grade framework.

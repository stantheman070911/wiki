## Net-new signal (reason to process)
1. **Three-level follower roadmap** with concrete effort estimates (0–1k is the hardest; ~50–100 videos to break 1k; new accounts grow slower). Each level's operating loop and graduation.
2. **The personal-brand "house":** foundation (niche + 5–7 subniches), interior (the three content pillars — educational, storytelling, authority), exterior (branding system: 2–3 colors, 2–3 fonts [primary/secondary/accent], 2–3 visual types = your editing style).
3. **Profile optimization, wrong vs right:** one bio link (not a Linktree — multiple links can cost ~50% of conversions), username = real/business name, tagline = name + tagline + searchable keyword, PFP = close-up on solid background (PFP Maker), bio = a 4-line framework.
4. **The 5× outlier rule:** a video with ≥5× the creator's follower count in views is a viral outlier; research via creator research, keyword research, and the reels recommendation tab; the Sortfeed Chrome extension sorts a profile's reels by views.
5. **The seven factors of a short:** topic → 3 hooks → value (measurable vs fluffy; own knowledge vs credited informational source) → script angle (7 angles) → CTA (ManyChat comment-to-DM) → video format (~11 engaging formats) → editing style.
6. **Doubling down / templatizing outliers:** analyze an outlier's seven components, transfer them three ways (A: small value tweaks; B: keep format, change the rest; C: keep everything, change the format). 90-day experiment → ~12M views, +83k followers.
7. **The 70/20/10 rule** (credited to "Caleb"): 70% proven niche outlier concepts, 20% double-downs on your own winners, 10% original experiments.
8. **Following vs personal brand:** a "following" (trends, aesthetic, B-roll+text) gives fluctuating results and demands every video be a masterpiece; a personal brand (educational + storytelling + authority) builds know-like-trust and yields consistent results. Plus an authenticity caveat: don't post anything "icky" you'll regret.
9. **Filming/editing ops:** script color-coding (black = script bullets, red = shot/action, green = editor notes; format at top), phone + Amazon tripod + mic, film one bullet at a time, folder-per-short with upload-between-shots hack, CapCut, caption = written hook / CTA / 3–5 keyword hashtags (hashtags as keywords, not a performance lever).

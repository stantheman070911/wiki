# Customer Acquisition, Offers, and Ads Playbook — Extraction Notes & Entry Map

*Source: four separate YouTube videos by Sabri Suby (founder of King Kong, a $250M+ agency; Shark Tank Australia), concatenated in the inbox dump. Raw transcript preserved alongside as `2026-07-10_Video_SabriSuby_CustomerAcquisitionOffersAndAdsPlaybook_RawTranscript.md`.*

---

## Nature of the source

Four distinct, long-form YouTube trainings glued into one file: (1) the CAC/LTV/payback-period math plus the Godfather Offer and Halo Strategy frameworks, (2) three marketing mistakes, (3) a Facebook ads / hyperdopamine-ad creative playbook, (4) seven business truths from 17 years running an agency. Dense enough, and covering enough distinct idea-clusters, to split into six entries rather than one.

## Overlap with existing entries (not re-processed as new entries, but cross-linked)

- Offer construction / value stacking / guarantees / scarcity → largely parallel to `The Value Equation and the Grand Slam Offer` (Hormozi). Kept as a separate entry (`The Godfather Offer`) because the structural emphasis differs — Suby's version centers on a credible *rationale* for the deal and a deliberate loss-leader-first-sale/profit-on-backend price structure that Hormozi's framing doesn't foreground the same way — but the two are cross-linked.
- "Give value before you ask" content-to-ads sequencing → adjacent to `Marrying Traffic and Monetization - The Give-Content, Ask-Ads Model` (Hormozi); cross-linked from the Buyer Awareness Pyramid entry rather than merged, since Suby's version is about awareness-stage segmentation and lead-magnet mechanics rather than the organic/paid channel split.
- Founder-level ownership framing → adjacent to `Accountability and the Blame-Power Law` (Hormozi); cross-linked from the Founder Is the Lid entry.

## Net-new signal → derived entries

**01-Business-Strategy/Finance-Capital-and-Exit/**
- **CAC, LTV, and Payback Period - The Three Numbers That Decide If You Win** — the core unit-economics equation, the 3:1 LTV:CAC target, "he who can spend the most to acquire a customer wins," payback-period mechanics and how to shorten it, and the discipline of spending time raising LTV rather than cutting CAC.

**01-Business-Strategy/Pricing-and-Offers/**
- **The Godfather Offer - A Seven-Part Framework for an Irresistible Offer** — rationale, value build, price-anchor/loss-leader-first-sale, payment plans, premiums, power guarantee, scarcity.

**01-Business-Strategy/Traffic-and-Acquisition/**
- **The Buyer Awareness Pyramid and the Halo Strategy - Marketing to the Market That Isn't Ready to Buy Yet** — the 3%/17%/20%/60% awareness pyramid, forum-foraging market research (Halo Strategy), give-away-the-secrets-sell-the-implementation, and tripling follow-up cadence.

**03-Tactics-and-Playbooks/Content-Creation-and-Distribution/**
- **Direct-Response Copywriting - NEWIF, the Nine Questions, and Three Conversion-Killing Mistakes** — the NEWIF formula, the nine questions every sales message must answer, and the buried-benefit/jargon-overload/claims-louder-than-proof mistakes.
- **Hyperdopamine Ads - Pattern Interrupt, Curiosity, and Native Ad Anatomy** — the pattern-interrupt + burning-intrigue + targeted-benefit formula, native ad styles, and ad anatomy (headline, image, lead-in copy, link description, body copy, CTA).

**04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models/Mindset-and-Identity/**
- **Founder Is the Lid - Who-Not-How and Planning Backward From the Goal** — the founder-as-ceiling framing, short-term sacrifice to build a team, and annual planning derived backward from a year-end goal.

## Caveats

- Single-narrator source; dollar figures and case results ($200M ad spend, $7.8B client sales, 2,000+ funnels) are the narrator's own claims, not independently verified.
- Some tactical advice (e.g., "avoid the words you/your in ad copy," specific CTA performance, exact character limits) is platform-and-era-specific and may not hold as ad platforms change.

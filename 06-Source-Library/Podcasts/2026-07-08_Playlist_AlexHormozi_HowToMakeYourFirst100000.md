## Net-new signal → derived entries
**01-Business-Strategy**
- **Barbell Pricing and the Unscalable Premium Offer** (Ep. 1) — expensive-to-few or cheap-to-many, the middle dies; sell your time 1:1 unscalably as the premium anchor; "when you have demand, cut supply"; anchor math (10 of 100 buyers on the premium ≈ 3× the profit, 100% margin); three frames to build the offer; the value drivers (dream outcome, perceived likelihood of achievement, speed/latency-beats-magnitude, effort/ease); describe the pain better than they can (mine book reviews).
- **The Five Advantages of a Perfect Business** (Ep. 7) — sticky (revenue retention; >100% NRR; churn curve → "get them to month 6"; resale-not-sales), expensive (gross margins), expansion (growing market), air (low operational complexity + low capex; ROIC), unique (moat: capex, trade secrets/patents, brand). Continuums, not binaries; fix retention first.

**03-Tactics-and-Playbooks**
- **The First $100k - The Complete Six-Step Roadmap** (Ep. 3) — cut costs → save time (5–9 chunks, the 4-4-4 promote/deliver/build day, maker vs manager) → research a paid skill (1-1-1) → learn (learning = same condition/new behavior; 10,000 iterations; first-party-data loop) → spend on tools/implementation/attempts → don't inflate lifestyle.
- **Using AI in Your Business - Train Like an Employee and the Department Playbook** (Ep. 4 + 6) — train AI like a new hire; observable behaviors over "magic/intuition" (pattern recognition); define what "good" looks like (12 rules + 16 samples → 5×); apples-to-apples vs a 10-year human process (John Henry); cloud-to-dirt knowledge; the screenshot-and-ask loop; department use cases (self-licking ice cream cone, risk reduction — PayPal/JPMorgan/Klarna/his own 5-agent 120k-ticket book launch); don't advertise that you use AI.

**04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models**
- **The Passion Myth - Proficiency, Pain Tolerance, and Frustration Tolerance** (Ep. 2) — "follow your passion" is true only in the vague; interests start you, proficiency continues you, pain tolerance finishes you; passionate about *excellence*, not the task; frustration tolerance is a learnable skill; reinforcement vs reward (the shock example); "all failure besides death is psychological"; Shoe Dog padlocks; nostalgia = memory minus pain; name the specific critic's voice.
- **Cringe Is a Status Signal - Shame, Guilt, and Documenting the Struggle** (Ep. 5) — shame = breaking others' rules, guilt = breaking your own, cringe = defensive status play (a sign your status is rising); anyone can make anything cringe by caring; the cringiest thing is fearing cringe; document the struggle (Kanye/Munger "deserve what you want"); don't judge chapter 1 against someone's chapter 25.

**05-Intelligence-and-Research**
- **Winning with AI - Workflow Thinking, BYOA, and Risk as the Last Human Job** (Ep. 4 + 6) — AI is the worst it'll ever be; ~20 hours to proficiency, first hour delayed for decades; adaptability survives (business Darwinism); role-based → workflow-based thinking (business as manufacturing belt; automate your own job); BYOA/BYOS future ("I am your entire marketing department," Anthropic's one-person team; erase "titleism"); the 18-month window; price sensitivity lags → margin + operational leverage; proof is the bottleneck of AI content; risk is the last thing humans get paid for; the AI barbell (AI-first + Bezos "won't-change" bets: bodies/health/consumables, leisure→entertainment; porn trickle-down; all-weather / hell-in-a-handbasket).

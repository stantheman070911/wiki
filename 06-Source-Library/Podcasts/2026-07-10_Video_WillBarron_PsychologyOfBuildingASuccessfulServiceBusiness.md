## Nature of the source

A single, two-part argument (not a multi-topic session): inconsistent revenue in service businesses is a psychological problem — an identity problem and a systems-design problem — before it's a tactics problem. Both parts point at the same underlying claim and were consolidated into one entry.

## Overlap with existing entries (not re-processed)

- Volume/feedback loops as the engine of sales skill → `Volume and Feedback - Sales as the Training Ground`.
- Removing friction and building process over relying on motivation → `Process as the Goal - The Zero-Follower Consistency Engine`, `Focus as Elimination - The Deep-Work Playbook`.

## Net-new signal → derived entry

**04-Frameworks-and-Mental-Models**
- **Identity and Systems - The Two Psychological Shifts Behind Consistent Revenue** — a three-system model of decision-making (automatic/identity system, reactive/emotional system, logical/forward-looking system); the identity shift from "service deliverer" to "business owner who owns selling too"; and the system shift from relying on willpower (finite, depletes across the day) to engineering the one avoided sales activity so it happens with minimal friction.

## Repeated examples worth preserving

- Jimali: a consultant who hated sales after a manipulative used-car-sales first job; reframed selling as helping a buyer decide, modeled a systematized process, and now proactively enjoys sales.
- Paul: overwhelmed by the idea of building "all the sales processes," instead ran a simple daily 30-minute block (pick a customer, find a look-alike contact, ask for a referral, book the meeting) and filled his calendar.
- The identity-gap writing exercise: current self-description + role vs. desired self-description + role; the gap is the work.

## Caveats

- Single-narrator source promoting the author's own mentorship program; the sales-assessment/mentorship pitch at the end was treated as promotional material and not extracted as an insight.
- "Discipline depletes across the day" is presented as settled research in the source; treat as a widely-cited but simplified account of ego depletion / decision fatigue, not a precise citation.

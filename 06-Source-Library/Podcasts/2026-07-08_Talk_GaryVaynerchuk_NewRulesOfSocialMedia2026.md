# The New Rules of Social Media (2026) — Gary Vaynerchuk — Extraction Notes & Entry Map

## Nature of the source
A landscape-shift keynote arguing that distribution has moved from a follow-graph to an interest-graph ("interest media"), that this collapses the advantage of incumbents and hands merit to the single best post, and that in an AI world the only defensible asset is brand — best built with a "barbell" of extreme technology plus 1950s human touch.

## Net-new signal (reason to process)
1. **Interest media, not social media.** Feeds now serve content by current interest, not by who you follow — so followers are no longer the gate to reach. Zero-follower post → 8.2M organic views. "Your third TikTok can change the course of your career." The single post's *merit now* outweighs your 15-year history.
2. **Organic-first, then amplify.** Post every sales/ad creative organically first; only put paid media behind the pieces that beat your view norm. Making an ad and running media cold is "over" because paid reach still fails if the creative isn't relevant.
3. **Volume + distributed accounts.** He posts hundreds/day (claimed 432 in one day) across many handles (GaryVee, "GaryVee on podcast," "Uncle V," a fresh "You're Somebody Now" account) — heading toward 1,000+ accounts — precisely because reach no longer needs followers.
4. **The attention barbell.** Extreme tech (AI, bots — people will ask an AI "who should I hire?") on one end; 1950s human touch on the other (call/text every past client just to say hello, zero transaction intent). Brand is the only defensible thing between them; do *both* ends, not the middle.
5. **Jab, jab, jab, right hook.** Value-first content (jabs/feints = free info) sets up the ask (right hook = the sale). All right hooks and the audience "ducks."
6. **Substack / written content** as an important new platform; information is a commodity, so people pay for *connection to the person*, and good writing becomes lead-gen through jabbing.
7. **Delivery objections dismissed.** You needn't be high-energy or camera-pretty; monotone works. "We are not in seventh grade anymore." Make 2026 an *action* year, not a thinking year.

## Notable quotes (verbatim)
- "We don't even live in social media anymore. We now live in interest media."
- "You don't need followers to get views."
- "Your third TikTok can fundamentally change the course of your career."
- "You should always post every piece of content you make for a sale or an ad organically first. If it does well in views compared to your norm, that's what you start running media on."
- "Brand is going to be the only thing that's defensible in this extreme technology world."
- "The whole world is about to be a barbell." (extreme tech vs 1950s)
- "Information is now useless. It is literally a commodity… yet people will pay for it if they connect with the person."
- "Take 2026 and make it an action year, not a thinking year."

## Caveats
- GaryVee is an outlier operator (large team, existing distribution); "432 posts/day" and "1,000 accounts" describe an agency-scale machine, not a solo baseline. The transferable claims are the *mechanisms* (interest-graph reach, organic-first testing, value-first jabs, the barbell), not the raw volume figures.

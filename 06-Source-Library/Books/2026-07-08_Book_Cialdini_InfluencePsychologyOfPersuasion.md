## Nature of the source
The foundational scientific text on compliance. Cialdini identifies why we say "yes" automatically: a set of deeply wired shortcuts that "compliance professionals" (salespeople, fundraisers, advertisers, con artists) exploit. One meta-mechanism (Chapter 1) plus six principles, one per chapter: reciprocation, commitment & consistency, social proof, liking, authority, scarcity.

## Key specifics (for accuracy)
- **"Click, whirr"** — a trigger feature fires a pre-recorded behavior tape (the turkey hen and the "cheep-cheep" of a stuffed polecat).
- **The "because" experiment (Langer)** — for a small favor, a *placebic* reason ("because I have to make copies") lifted compliance nearly as much as a real reason; the word "because" itself was the trigger.
- **Contrast principle** — judging the second item relative to the first (show the expensive house/suit first; then the cheaper item seems small).
- **Reciprocation** — free samples, the Hare Krishna flower, the $5-for-$3 experiment; **rejection-then-retreat / door-in-the-face** (Boy Scout $5 ticket → $1 chocolate bar) also triggers reciprocation *and* contrast, and produces more responsibility and satisfaction.
- **Commitment & consistency** — foot-in-the-door (small "safe-driver" sticker → big ugly-sign yard request); commitments that are **active, public, effortful, and freely chosen** stick; the **lowball** (give a reason to decide, then remove it).
- **Social proof** — canned laughter (works best on poor jokes); strongest under **uncertainty** and **similarity**; pluralistic ignorance/bystander effect; the Werther effect (publicized suicides → copycats).
- **Liking** — physical attractiveness halo; similarity; compliments; contact & cooperation (good/bad-cop; Tupperware party = liking the host); association (weathercasters blamed for weather; luncheon technique).
- **Authority** — Milgram obedience; the *symbols* suffice: titles, clothes (uniforms), and trappings (a luxury car).
- **Scarcity** — limited-number and deadline tactics; **psychological reactance** (banned information becomes more desirable); scarcity from newly-imposed restriction and from **competition** is most potent.

## Notable quotes (verbatim)

## Caveats
- These are documented compliance mechanisms; Cialdini frames each with an explicit **defense**, because the same shortcuts that serve us are exploited against us. Every derived entry preserves the defense.
- Use ethically: the book's own stance is that exploiting these against someone's interest invites justified retaliation and erodes trust.

# The Almanack of Naval Ravikant (Eric Jorgenson) — Extraction Notes & Entry Map

*Source: book, "The Almanack of Naval Ravikant: A Guide to Wealth and Happiness," by Eric Jorgenson (2020), built from Naval Ravikant's tweets, transcripts, and talks (~49,000 words). Free public-service book (Navalmanack.com). Original PDF archived alongside at `2026-07-08_Book_EricJorgenson_TheAlmanackOfNavalRavikant.pdf`.*

---

## Nature of the source
Naval's collected philosophy in two parts — **Wealth** (Building Wealth + Building Judgment) and **Happiness** (Learning Happiness + Saving Yourself + Philosophy). It is the canonical long-form of the "How to Get Rich (without getting lucky)" tweetstorm plus his happiness writing.

## Overlap with existing entries (large — NOT re-processed)
The wealth thesis and the decision/happiness *philosophy* are already captured in the vault from the Modern Wisdom / "How to Get Rich" material:
- Seek wealth not money/status; three games; rent-time-vs-equity; four kinds of luck; specific knowledge; accountability; the three leverages (labor, capital, **code & media** = permissionless leverage); judgment; long-term games with long-term people; productize yourself; escape competition through authenticity; business-model leverage (economies of scale, zero marginal cost, network effects, principal-agent) → **`財富公式：專屬知識、槓桿與判斷力`**.
- Happiness as satisfaction/absence of desire; desire = a contract to be unhappy; the boredom→want→suffer→habituate loop; pride as the costliest trait; freedom & attention; mind-management (stress vs anxiety); decision-making (intuition decides / reason rationalizes, "if you can't decide the answer is no," the secretary/33% rule, iterations-not-hours, the three big decisions: who/what/where); rational optimism; self-esteem; love; parenting; peace → **`決策、幸福與心智管理`**.
- Naval's contrarian/AI/prediction takes → `納瓦爾的預測與逆勢觀點`, `AI、資料與內容的賭注理論`.

## Net-new signal (reason to process) → derived entries
Two clusters are under-covered by the existing entries and are processed here:

1. **Learn to Love to Read + How to Think Clearly** (Building Judgment). The reading-as-meta-skill teaching and the "foundations over news, read the originals, understand the basics" epistemics are not in the vault. → entry **Learn to Love to Read - Foundations, Clear Thinking, and Mental Models**.
2. **Happiness as a trainable skill + Saving Yourself** (the *practice* side — presence, peace, habits, meditation, diet/sleep/exercise). The existing happiness entry covers the philosophy; the practical, skill/self-care angle is net-new. → entry **Happiness Is a Skill - Presence, Peace, and Self-Care**.

## Notable quotes (verbatim)
- "Read what you love until you love to read."
- "The genuine love for reading itself, when cultivated, is a superpower."
- "The number of books completed is a vanity metric… Focus on new concepts with predictive power."
- "Read the greats in math, science, and philosophy. Ignore your contemporaries and news."
- "To think clearly, understand the basics. If you're memorizing advanced concepts without being able to re-derive them, you're lost."
- "If they wrote it to make money, don't read it."
- "The three big ones in life are wealth, health, and happiness. We pursue them in that order, but their importance is reverse."
- "Peace is happiness at rest, and happiness is peace in motion."
- "Happiness is a choice… If you believe it's a choice, you can start working on it."
- "Easy choices, hard life. Hard choices, easy life."
- "You're just a monkey with a plan."

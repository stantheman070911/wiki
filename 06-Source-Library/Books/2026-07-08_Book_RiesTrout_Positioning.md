# Positioning: The Battle for Your Mind (Al Ries & Jack Trout) — Extraction Notes & Entry Map

*Source: book (markdown transcript), "Positioning: The Battle for Your Mind," by Al Ries and Jack Trout (concept coined 1972; classic marketing text, ~51,800 words, 25 chapters + Kotler foreword). Retained in `_Inbox` as a `.md`; the markdown is the archive.*

---

## Nature of the source
The founding text of positioning — the discipline that precedes the four Ps (Kotler: R, STP, *then* P before the four Ps). Its thesis: in an overcommunicated society you win not by improving the product but by occupying a simple, defensible position in the prospect's *mind*.

## Net-new signal (reason to process) → derived entries
The core positioning doctrine is not yet in the vault. Four clusters:
1. **The thesis** — positioning is done to the mind, not the product; the overcommunicated society; the oversimplified message; own one word; perception is reality (outside-in). → *Positioning - Own a Word in the Prospect's Mind*.
2. **Getting in** — be first, or position *against* the leader; the mental "ladders" (~7 rungs); you can't go head-to-head with an owned position. → folded into the thesis entry and the leader/follower entry.
3. **Leader vs follower play** — leader: get there first, then defend; follower: *cherchez le creneau* (find the hole — size, price, gender, age, distribution); repositioning the competition (Tylenol→aspirin, Avis #2, 7Up uncola). → *Leader, Follower, and the Hole in the Mind*.
4. **The line-extension trap** — hanging one name on many products dilutes the position; share-of-mind > share-of-market; the shopping-list test. → *The Line-Extension Trap*.
5. **Positioning yourself / your career** — find a horse to ride; be a specialist not a generalist; the six questions; outside-in thinking. → *Positioning Yourself - Find a Horse to Ride*.

## Notable quotes / specifics (verbatim)
- "Positioning is not what you do to a product… Positioning is what you do to the mind of the prospect."
- "The only reality that counts is what's already in the prospect's mind."
- "In communication, less is more… the best approach is the oversimplified message."
- "Cherchez le creneau." (Look for the hole — then fill it.)
- "If there are no creneaus left, you have to create one by repositioning the competition." (Tylenol → aspirin)
- "Avis is only No. 2 in rent-a-cars… We try harder." / "Seven-Up: the uncola."
- "Prospects don't buy, they choose."
- "Share of mind > share of market." (Scott paper: big share, no owned position)
- "Find yourself a horse to ride… success in life is based more on what others can do for you than on what you can do for yourself."
- The six questions: What position do you own? / want to own? / Whom must you outgun? / Do you have enough money? / Can you stick it out? / Do you match your position?

## Caveats
- Examples are 1980s brands (Sanka, Rheingold, Protein 21); the *principles* are durable, the case brands dated.
- Positioning restricts creativity by design — creativity is only valuable when subordinated to the positioning objective.

## Nature of the source
An action-oriented workbook of eleven chapters split into two halves: brand/content strategy (define your brand, brand story, posting cadence, storytelling, experimentation) and team-building (job descriptions, hiring funnel, FTE vs contractors, onboarding, 1:1s, culture). Delivered as fill-in worksheets and step-by-step playbooks.

## Key specifics
- **Brand Journey (4 questions):** What do I want to have happen? → What do I have to be known for? → What do I have to do to be known for that? → What do I have to learn to do those things?
- **3-Part Brand Story:** (1) The Catalyst (why the brand exists / what needed to change — Nike/Phil Knight saw a performance-shoe gap), (2) The Core Truth (what makes you different, a conviction against the market — Russ on artists owning their music), (3) The Proof (reinforce the identity over and over — GaryVee proving content volume for 15+ years).
- **Waterfall steps:** pillar content → extract micro-content ("mining for gold": 30–60s clips, standout quotes, key takeaways, contrarian takes) → platform-native formats (Shorts/TikTok/Reels, X thread 5–10 tweets, LinkedIn post/carousel, IG Stories, email) → strategic distribution (pillar first; micro in 24–48h; engage & iterate; repurpose winners).
- **Storytelling structure:** Hook (2 seconds) → Problem (stakes) → Journey (the messy middle, "progress not perfection") → Lesson (make it useful).
- **Hiring funnel:** define the role in one sentence (success at 3–6 months) → filter (optional video application → screening call on expectations → skill test on *real* work, no hypotheticals) → decision.

## Caveats
- A lead magnet for a paid/free course; light on depth, heavy on worksheets. The frameworks (Waterfall, 3-part story, hiring funnel) are the durable value.
- Much of the brand-definition and storytelling material restates concepts already in the vault; processing is limited to the genuinely additive frameworks.

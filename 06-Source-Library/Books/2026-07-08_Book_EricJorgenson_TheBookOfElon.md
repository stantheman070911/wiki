# The Book of Elon (Eric Jorgenson) — Extraction Notes & Entry Map

*Source: book, "The Book of Elon: A Guide to Purpose and Success — Elon Musk's Most Useful Ideas, in His Own Words," by Eric Jorgenson (2026; foreword by Naval Ravikant; same series as the Almanack). ~68,800 words. Original PDF archived alongside at `2026-07-08_Book_EricJorgenson_TheBookOfElon.pdf`.*

## Nature of the source
Musk's transferable operating principles in his own words, organized into: Pursue Purpose (physicist thinking, first principles, engineering), Ultra Hardcore Work (teams, org design, The Algorithm, urgency, manufacturing), Building Companies (Zip2/PayPal/Tesla/SpaceX narratives), and On Behalf of Humanity (abundance, existential risk, Mars). A "69 Core Musk Methods" appendix distills the lot.

## Net-new signal (reason to process) → derived entries
The Musk operating frameworks are not in the vault. Processed (transferable frameworks; biography and Mars-colonization content noted but not entry-ified):
1. **First-principles thinking** — reason up from fundamental truths, not by analogy; think in limits; obsess over truth; aspire to be less wrong. → *First-Principles Thinking - Reason from Physics, Not Analogy*.
2. **The Algorithm** — the five-step engineering/process method (question requirements → delete → simplify → accelerate → automate), with corollaries ("every requirement has a name," "if you're not adding back 10%, you're not deleting enough," "the best part is no part"). → *The Algorithm - Musk's Five-Step Process Method*.
3. **The factory is the product** — the machine that builds the machine; manufacturing is the hard part and the moat; attack the constraint; give people more for less. → *The Factory Is the Product - Manufacturing as the Moat*.
4. **Maniacal urgency** — speed as offense and defense; parallelize; break down the "impossible"; set aggressive timelines. → *Maniacal Urgency - Speed, Parallelism, and Aggressive Timelines*.
5. **Special-forces teams / flat orgs** — recruit for exceptional ability, retain only special forces, feedback over feelings, remove organizational boundaries, simple communication, innovation needs permission to fail. → *Special-Forces Teams and Flat Organizations*.
6. **Companies as philanthropy** — building a company that solves a real problem at scale beats donating; companies create wealth for all; be useful; purpose as the driver. → *Companies as Philanthropy - Purpose and Building for Humanity*.

## The Algorithm (verbatim, order matters)
1. Make your requirements less dumb. 2. Try very hard to delete the part or process. 3. Simplify or optimize. 4. Accelerate. 5. Automate.
- "Your requirements are definitely dumb… Requirements from smart people are the most dangerous." Every requirement must come from a *named person*, not a department.
- "If you're not adding deleted things back in 10% of the time, you're not deleting enough."
- "The best part is no part; the best process is no process." (Method #26)
- Musk's own mistake: he repeatedly ran the steps *backward* (automated, accelerated, simplified, then deleted) — e.g. the fiberglass battery mats that a $2M robot installed for "fire safety" that a test proved unnecessary.

## Caveats
- Musk is a polarizing figure; the book is a curated, favorable "in his own words" compilation. Entries capture the *methods*, not an endorsement of the person, and note where claims are self-serving.
- The Mars/colonization, existential-risk, and company-history narratives are recorded here but not turned into entries (biography and speculation over transferable method).

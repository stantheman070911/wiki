# The Seven Factors of a Short - Topic to Editing

## One-line summary
Every short-form video is built from seven components — topic, hooks, value, script angle, CTA, video format, and editing style — and writing them deliberately (not "throwing spaghetti at the wall") is what makes a script reliably work.

## Context
This is the atomic unit beneath any Instagram growth roadmap: a repeatable checklist for scripting one short so quality is engineered rather than hoped for. It also turns one idea into many via interchangeable angles and formats.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
**The seven factors:**

1. **Topic** — pick one of your subniches as the subject of this video.
2. **Hooks (×3)** — the first 3 seconds across three channels: **verbal** (what you hear), **visual** (what you see), **written** (what you read on screen). Don't invent them cold — reuse *proven viral outlier* hooks. (See the 5× rule in [[Double-Down and the 70-20-10 Rule - Templatizing Viral Outliers]].)
3. **Value** — the "meat"/big takeaway. Sourced from your **own knowledge and experience** (preferred, most authentic) or a credited **informational source** (study, article, another video). Make it **measurable** (hyper-specific, clear) not **fluffy** (vague). Decide the one actionable takeaway the viewer leaves with.
4. **Script angle** — how the idea is structured; one idea becomes seven via the **seven angles**: (1) tutorial (step/framework/acronym), (2) comparison (two methods → same result), (3) mythbust / common mistake, (4) do-vs-don't / right-vs-wrong, (5) educational tip/hack, (6) transformation (before→after + how), (7) challenge (turn a niche task into a completed challenge). Any angle can be delivered as educational *or* storytelling content.
5. **CTA** — the end ask, aimed at the main goal: **followers** or **leads** (product/service/freebie). For leads, use a comment-to-DM automation (ManyChat): viewer comments a trigger word → auto-DM'd the link. Set the automation up in advance.
6. **Video format** — the engaging way it's filmed, to hold attention and lift watch time. A menu: talking back-and-forth (smart vs dumb character), visual/props, voiceover, multitasking (teach while cooking/cleaning/makeup), setting changes, shot/angle change (new shot every ~2s), clone (double yourself to compare two things), whiteboard, one/multi-person Q&A, green screen, reaction (react to a mega-viral niche video, then give your expert take). Adjust the script to fit the chosen format.
7. **Editing style** — the text, colors, visuals, and music, applied from your pre-set branding "exterior."

## Tactics / how to apply
- Run the seven factors as a literal checklist for every script; don't skip the hook research or the "measurable value" test.
- Turn one topic into a week of content by cycling it through the seven angles.
- Pre-decide the CTA and build the ManyChat automation before publishing.
- Choose the format before writing so the script is built for it, then batch-write a week-to-a-month of scripts at once.
- Prefer your own knowledge for value; if you must borrow, credit the source in-video to stay authentic.

## Notable examples
- Jeff Nippard's fitness shorts illustrate value sourcing: some break down a *study* (informational source), others share his own eating/experience (own knowledge) — both produce measurable takeaways.
- Angle transformation: a single "abs" topic spun into seven distinct video ideas via the seven angles.

# The Content City Map - Goal, Signal, Content

## One-line summary
Plan content backwards from business goals using a subway-map metaphor: your *why* is the central station, your goals are destinations, "signals" are the lines that carry people there, your audience are the passengers, content formats are the stops, and your content calendar is the timetable вЂ” and every piece is judged against the one metric that goal actually needs.

## Context

## The map (work backwards: goal в†’ signal в†’ content)
Picture your brand as a city (e.g. New York) that people visit for a reason:
1. **Grand Central = your *why*.** The anchor everything routes back to (Grace's: "be the intersection for creators and brands"). It's why people come to your city.
2. **Destinations = your goals.** Pick ~3 per season/quarter (e.g. 10Г— inbound speaking inquiries; sign a book deal; grow YouTube 10kв†’100k).
3. **Lines = your *signals*.** What has to be true to carry someone from the *why* to a destination вЂ” the belief you must build (e.g. for speaking: increase credibility, demonstrate speaking ability, own unique topics).
4. **Passengers = the audience** for each line вЂ” the specific people who must ride it (event bookers; literary agents; your target followers). Different lines carry different people.
5. **Stops = content formats.** The pieces that move a passenger along a line (podcast cut-downs for credibility; short-form on a niche topic; written LinkedIn posts).
6. **Timetable = your content plan.** How the trains run вЂ” e.g. two posts per line per week вЂ” reviewed monthly: did passengers move down the line (more inquiries, a book conversation, closer to 100k)? If yes, keep going; if no, change the timetable.

As you grow, you add lines and the map becomes a network serving many goals at once (as Diary of a CEO did across podcast, book, merch, shows).

## Key insights
- **Goal в†’ signal в†’ content, always backwards.** Most people pick the content they want to make and then ask how to measure it. Reverse it: name the goal, name the belief (signal) it needs, then choose formats that build that belief.
- **Know exactly what you're measuring вЂ” before you post.** Every piece must have a business goal and a success metric set *in advance*, or you become "a paper bag in the wind." A reel can "overperform on follows" (Instagram's metric) yet fail *your* metric (e.g. conversation) вЂ” and if you weren't measuring follows, that success is noise. When something fails your metric, that's the interesting part: ask *why* and improve.
- **Consistency Г— experimentation is the growth formula.** Consistency alone plateaus; you only get hockey-stick, compounding growth by consistently trying new things вЂ” new features, trends, formats вЂ” and keeping what works.
- **Virality is a sugar rush.** Empty views ("forgettable seconds") feel great and crash; the goal is repeatedly reaching the *right* people and building a relationship, not views for their own sake.

## Application
1. Write your one *why*. Set ~3 destinations (goals) for the quarter.
2. For each, define the signal (belief to build), the passengers (who must see it), and 2вЂ“3 content formats that carry them.
3. Assign a single success metric per line *before* publishing; build a weekly timetable; review monthly and ask "did passengers move?" вЂ” if not, why, and adjust.
4. Run multiple lines at once (some for growth, some for retention/conversation), each measured on its own goal.

# The Kaleidoscope Process - Squeeze Every Winning Ad

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[03-Tactics-and-Playbooks/Tactics and Playbooks Index|Tactics and Playbooks]]

## One-line summary
When an ad wins, don't just admire it and move on — remix that one winner a dozen superficial ways (backdrop, props, filters, pacing, re-shoots, same-hook variants) to extract 10× or 100× more return from the creative you've already proven, because a tiny fraction of ads produce almost all the profit.

## Context
Advertising returns follow a power law: the top ~5% of ads make ~95% of the money. Volume exists to *find* those rare winners (see [[More, Better, New - The Scaling Priority Sequence|More, Better, New]] and [[01-Business-Strategy/Traffic-and-Acquisition/The Creative Volume Engine - Ads Are an At-Bats Game|The Creative Volume Engine]]) — but the mistake most operators make is under-milking a winner once they have one. Hormozi's "kaleidoscope process" is the systematic way to reuse a proven ad by changing surface features while keeping the winning core (hook + message) intact.

## The ten remixes
Take a proven winner and generate variants by changing one surface element at a time — the message and hook stay the same:

1. **Backdrop / background color** — re-record against a different backdrop or swap it via green screen.
2. **Props** — add or change physical objects or on-screen visuals.
3. **Re-enact it** — have the same person (or you) simply perform the exact ad again in a different shirt.
4. **Reorder** — rearrange the sections of the ad; it often still converts.
5. **Visual filters** — black-and-white, sepia, high/low contrast — same message, different look.
6. **Visual effects** — overlays, whizbangs, motion graphics.
7. **Fonts / captions** — change the caption style and typography.
8. **Pacing / speed** — run portions at 1.2×–1.5× or slow sections down.
9. **Music** — add or change the track.
10. **Same hook, new body** — keep the proven hook (you *know* it works) and re-record a different back half behind it.

## Key insights
- **Squeeze the winners.** The single highest-leverage advertising habit: when something crushes, don't chase a brand-new idea — extract maximum mileage from what already works. New creative from scratch is expensive and uncertain; a remix of a winner starts from a known result.
- **Keep the proven core fixed.** The hook and message are what won; the kaleidoscope only varies presentation, so each variant re-serves the same value to a slightly different slice of audience (or the same audience with fresh framing) without gambling on a new concept.
- **This is a power-law tactic.** Because a handful of ads carry the account, doubling or 10×-ing the reach of one proven winner is worth far more than another batch of untested concepts.

## Application
- Maintain a "winners" shelf; the moment an ad clears your bar, queue it for the kaleidoscope rather than retiring it.
- Batch-produce 5–10 remixes per winner using the list above, changing exactly one surface element per variant so you can see what still holds.
- Re-run the same-hook variant first — it's the cheapest and most reliable, since the hook did most of the work.
- Fold this into the "more" stage of scaling: squeezing winners is how you do *more* of what works before ever making something new.

# The Whisper-Tease-Shout Launch

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[03-Tactics-and-Playbooks/Tactics and Playbooks Index|Tactics and Playbooks]] → [[03-Tactics-and-Playbooks/Content-Creation-and-Distribution/Content Creation and Distribution Map|Content Creation and Distribution]]

## One-line summary
Launch anything by escalating attention over time in three phases — whisper to build curiosity, tease to reveal value, shout to drive action — front-loading all the work so the launch day is plug-and-play.

## Context
A launch concentrates demand into a window: promote before the product is buyable, then sell to all the engaged leads at once. Hormozi presents this in the affiliates chapter of *$100M Leads* because nothing activates an [[Affiliate Armies - Recruiting Businesses to Sell for You|affiliate army]] like a launch — but it works for any product, offer, cohort, or content drop. The three phases map cleanly onto the three parts of an ad: call-out → value → CTA. A useful real-world model is a movie release (year-out teaser → longer trailers → release-week saturation).

## Framework / model
### The three phases
1. **Whisper — curiosity (the call-out).** Reveal *nothing* concrete; hint that something big is coming and show behind-the-scenes of making it. The longer the build, the more the audience values it ("show your work"). Embed questions in their minds, then say "not yet." Can start years out.
2. **Tease — value (the elements of value).** Start satisfying the curiosity: announce the date, reveal the product, and show the value elements (dream outcome, likelihood, speed, low effort) with concrete examples.
3. **Shout — action (the CTA).** Give specific, short, repeated calls to action, now stacked with bonuses, scarcity, urgency, and first-mover guarantees. Saturate.

### Cadence (escalating frequency toward launch)
| Phase | Timeline | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Whisper | until ~60 days out | every 4–6 weeks, then every 2–3 weeks |
| Tease | ~30 → 3 days out | ~1×/week, then 2×/week |
| Shout | ~3 days → launch | ≥2×/day, then every few hours, then every 30 min in the final ~2 hours |

## Tactics / how to apply
- Do the work for your promoters/affiliates ahead of time so their part is plug-and-play on launch day.
- Whisper by documenting the build (drafts, iterations, early-morning work) rather than describing the product — process footage manufactures curiosity for free.
- In the tease phase, use a "what / who / win" structure: what it is, who it's for, and the outcome — with as many concrete examples as you can.
- In the shout phase, keep each message short and single-CTA; a confused mind doesn't buy, so ask for exactly one action per touch.
- Layer bonuses/scarcity/urgency only in the shout phase (see [[Scarcity and Urgency - Engineering Demand with Supply and Deadlines]]) — deployed earlier they lose their punch.

## Notable examples
- Hormozi's own book launches run this exact sequence — years of whispering (draft photos, framework sketches), weeks of teasing (the promise of limitless leads, worked examples), and days of shouting (register-now reminders, launch-only bonuses).
- Prestige Labs used a launch to activate ~400 gym affiliates, converting a flat first three weeks into ~$450K/week by month's end.

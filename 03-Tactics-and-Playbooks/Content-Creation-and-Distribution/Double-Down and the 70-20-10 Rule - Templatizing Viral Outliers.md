# Double-Down and the 70/20/10 Rule - Templatizing Viral Outliers

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[03-Tactics-and-Playbooks/Tactics and Playbooks Index|Tactics and Playbooks]] → [[03-Tactics-and-Playbooks/Content-Creation-and-Distribution/Content Creation and Distribution Map|Content Creation and Distribution]]

## One-line summary
Find viral outliers (≥5× a creator's follower count in views), template them by transferring their components, and structure each month's content 70/20/10 across proven concepts, your own double-downs, and fresh experiments.

## Context
Once past the initial grind, growth is driven less by novelty than by systematically reusing what already worked — both other creators' proven concepts and your own outliers. This is the engine of the 1k–10k and 10k–100k levels.

## Key insights
- **The 5× outlier rule.** A "viral outlier" is a video with at least five times the creator's follower count in views — a small/mid creator drastically outperforming their norm. Because it already went viral, its hook/concept has a strong chance of going viral again.
- **Research outliers where virality is hardest.** Do outlier research on Instagram (the hardest platform to go viral on) so what works there travels well.
- **Don't throw spaghetti — reuse proven hooks.** Pull verbal, written, and visual hooks from outliers rather than inventing them cold.
- **Top creators recreate their winners.** When a big creator hits a viral video, they remake it repeatedly with small adjustments — "templatizing."

## Framework / model (if applicable)
**Finding outliers (three methods):**
1. **Creator research** — search your video topic on Explore, open the Accounts and Reels tabs, find successful niche creators, then surface each one's most-viral videos (manually, or with the Sortfeed Chrome extension: on their profile → Reels → sort by views → export CSV). Keep hooks meeting the 5× rule.
2. **Keyword research** — search your topic on Explore → Reels tab → scroll and save 5×-rule videos.
3. **Reels recommendation tab** — after doing (1) and (2), your Explore/For-You feed surfaces hidden gems around your topic; grab the 5×-rule ones.

**Doubling down (templatizing an outlier):**
1. **Find** your outlier — a video far above your average (e.g. 10k views on a 1k-average page).
2. **Analyze** its seven components (topic, hooks, value, angle, CTA, format, editing).
3. **Double down** by transferring components three ways: **(A)** keep most components, make small adjustments or a small change in value each time; **(B)** keep the format/general structure, change almost everything else; **(C)** keep everything the same, change only the *format* it's presented in.

**The 70/20/10 rule** (credited to "Caleb") — for each month's batch:
- **70%** — proven concepts: niche outliers' hooks/topics you reuse because they're already validated.
- **20%** — double-downs on *your own* best performers (templatizing your outliers).
- **10%** — original experiments from scratch: new ideas of your own.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Set your outlier threshold at 5× follower count and collect a running bank of qualifying hooks by topic.
- When a video beats your average markedly, immediately template it using modes A/B/C rather than moving on.
- Build every monthly batch to 70/20/10; as you mature, the 10% experiments become your next proven concepts.
- Use the Edits app to identify your five best and worst videos before deciding what to double down on.

## Notable examples
- The creator ran a 90-day double-down experiment (templatizing outliers) and reports ~12 million views and +83,000 followers — attributing it to disciplined reuse rather than constant novelty.
- Mode C in practice: take a video that worked and re-shoot the identical value/hook in a new engaging format.

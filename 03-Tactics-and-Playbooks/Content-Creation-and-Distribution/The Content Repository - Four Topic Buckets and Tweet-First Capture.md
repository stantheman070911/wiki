# The Content Repository - Four Topic Buckets and Tweet-First Capture

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[03-Tactics-and-Playbooks/Tactics and Playbooks Index|Tactics and Playbooks]] → [[03-Tactics-and-Playbooks/Content-Creation-and-Distribution/Content Creation and Distribution Map|Content Creation and Distribution]]

## One-line summary
Never run out of content by capturing ideas in real time into a single repository (Hormozi uses tweets as the source of everything) and drawing topics from four buckets — far past, recent past, real-time, and manufactured.

## Context
"What do I post?" is the recurring bottleneck. Hormozi's answer is a capture habit plus a topic taxonomy that makes ideas both interesting and effectively endless, feeding a repurposing system that multiplies each idea across platforms.

## Key insights
- **Capture in real time or lose it.** Ideas must land in a repository the moment they occur; retroactively you won't remember them on the spot. Hormozi tweets an idea in two seconds — tweets are "the life source of everything." (Before going public at Gym Launch, the same habit lived as one running email thread to himself of lessons and failures.)
- **One idea, many dips.** A captured tweet becomes the seed for recorded videos (the team pulls up his tweets: "what was this about?"), becomes an Instagram post, becomes a Reel, and can be read aloud to become a video. Double, triple, quadruple, quintuple dips on a single capture.
- **Your calendar is a content archive.** Look back at every meeting and where you spent time; each is a story ("a client's order bump wasn't working, here's what we did"). You won't surface these unless you look retroactively.
- **Niche content is where product-market fit lives.** The buyers come from the niche content even when it gets fewer views; wider content is further top-of-funnel that introduces the right people, who then go deeper into the niche videos.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
**The four topic buckets:**
1. **Far past** — the big lessons and belief-shifts that are valuable to someone else.
2. **Recent past** — this week's calendar: meetings, problems solved, things learned.
3. **Real-time** — the tweet-dump / notes capture as ideas occur throughout the day.
4. **Manufactured** — deliberately staged, entertainment-leaning content ("I lived on $100 for a month," "I started a Skool community from scratch").

**The niche-wide pendulum.** Keep most content on-topic for your avatar; sprinkle in wider pieces as top-of-funnel at roughly **3:1 to 4:1 (niche:wide)**. Wider pieces pull more of the right people in (Hormozi's "lessons to my younger self" out-drew case studies ~3:1 and broadened the funnel); the niche pieces then convert them. Expect volume to drop when you swing wide, and swing back.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Choose one always-with-you capture channel (tweets, a notes app, a running email thread) and dump every idea into it immediately.
- In each recording session, pull your recent captures and let the team turn each into multiple platform-specific assets (video → Reel → post → written).
- Once a week, scan your calendar for meetings and problems that make natural stories.
- Maintain all four buckets so you always have far-past lessons, recent-past stories, real-time captures, and a few manufactured pieces queued.
- Run content ~3–4:1 niche-to-wide; treat wide pieces as funnel entry, not as the main line.

## Notable examples
- Hormozi's tweet → Reel → video → post multi-dip: a single two-second tweet becomes the source for the week's content across platforms.
- The pre-public "lessons and failures" email thread — the same repository idea before Twitter.
- The "lessons to my younger self" videos drew the whole workshop room despite ~1/3 the views of his case studies — top-of-funnel that feeds the niche.

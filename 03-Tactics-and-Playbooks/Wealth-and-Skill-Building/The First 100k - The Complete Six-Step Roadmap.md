# The First $100k - The Complete Six-Step Roadmap

## One-line summary
Bank your first $100k in six moves: cut costs to free capital, reclaim time, research a skill people already pay for, actually learn it through iterations, spend on tools/help/attempts, and don't inflate your lifestyle — because $100k in the bank is what finally lets you think long-term.

## Context
Hormozi says he felt wealthiest not at his $46.2M exit but at his first $100k, when he stopped doing math on groceries and could think past next month's rent (Maslow's hierarchy — you can't chase a vision while you owe rent tomorrow). This is the full six-step version of that climb; it extends the defense-first saving logic in [[The First 100k - Defense-Before-Offense Playbook]].

## Framework / model (if applicable)
**Step 1 — Cut all costs (to take risk).** All costs. No eating out; discount groceries only; two years of clothing is what's in your bag; live with family or cheap roommates; a paid-off clunker. The point is to convert wasted spend into reinvestable cash flow. (Hormozi paid $300–400/month splitting a room for years even after making real money.)

**Step 2 — Save time.** A 9–5 isn't killing your dream; wasting the two 4-hour blocks around it (5–9am, 5–9pm) is. If you have no day job, run a **4-4-4 day**: 4 hours **promote** (nobody can pay you if they don't know about you), 4 hours **deliver** (give buyers what you promised), 4 hours **build** (curate opportunities, then prioritize the highest-return one). Then protect focus with **maker vs manager** time: a maker's perfect day is an empty calendar (deep build), a manager's is packed with touchpoints/decisions — block them separately, because task-switching is the biggest productivity killer (Hormozi makes Mondays his manager day). Focus is achieved by subtraction, not addition.

**Step 3 — Research a skill people already pay for.** Don't guess; find existing demand. B2B: the functions a business already buys (content, outreach, funnels — any one can be a >$1M business). B2C: print your bank/credit-card statement and see what you actually pay for. Then narrow with the **1-1-1 rule** — one product/service, one avatar, one channel, until $1M. (See [[少即是多的擴張系統：Traffic-Systems-Skills 診斷、1-1-1 與萬能前門]].)

**Step 4 — Spend time learning.** Learning = **same condition, new behavior**; if nothing you do changes, you're not learning (a slip-test for whether content is useful: can you turn it into "what do I do now?"). It's **10,000 iterations, not 10,000 hours** — iterations are a proxy for feedback loops, and no feedback means no learning (a post that flops is still feedback). Fastest path: hire someone great 1:1. **The first-party-data loop:** gather high volume → isolate the top 10% of outcomes → find what they share that the bottom 90% lack → identify the few details that matter (test them one at a time) → avoid the 90%'s mistakes, copy the 10% → repeat for the next hundred reps.

**Step 5 — Spend money in the right places.** Three buckets: **tools** (a CRM, landing pages, Skool at $9/mo — don't rebuild what exists), **implementation help** (courses, communities, tutoring — hugely underrated), and **trial attempts** (ad spend, editing software — accept minor investments that give leverage). Saving alone won't get you to $100k; you must also *do*.

**Step 6 — Don't increase your lifestyle.** Be rich, not look rich. This is $100k in the *bank*, not revenue; everything minus food and shelter is profit to bank. "The day you stop spending money on learning is the day you decide you don't want to make more."

## Tactics / how to apply
- Drive fixed living costs to the floor and keep them there even as income rises; bank the delta.
- Reclaim the 5–9 blocks; if full-time on your own thing, run the 4-4-4 day and separate maker/manager time.
- Validate the skill against real spending (statements), then commit to 1-1-1 until $1M.
- Judge every learning input by whether it changes your behavior; chase iterations and feedback, not hours.
- Deploy saved cash into tools, tutoring/implementation, and paid trial attempts — then increase active income and leave your lifestyle flat.

## Notable examples
- Hormozi split a bedroom in a six-bedroom house at ~$400/month while building; at ~$20k/month income from his first gym he still paid $400 rent to fund expansion and learning.
- The first-$100k moment: telling Leila "we did it — we could do nothing for three and a half years," the first time he could think long-term.

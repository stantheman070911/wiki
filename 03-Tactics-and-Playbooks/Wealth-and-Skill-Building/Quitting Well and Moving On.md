# Quitting Well and Moving On

## One-line summary
A generative quit converts the final months of one job into reputation, relationships, operating knowledge, and momentum for the next.

## Context
Bridge burning destroys future options, while a perfunctory two-week exit wastes accumulated trust. Designing the quit as a transition preserves a professional network and helps both the organization and the successor succeed.

## Key insights
- **Choose quitting; do not let quitting choose you.** Framing departure as a purposeful decision restores agency and produces a better story.
- **Try redesign and ask the boss first.** The problem may be fixable or caused by context you cannot see.
- **A new job is easier to land while employed.** It also protects financial stability during an uncertain search.
- **Leave the campsite better than you found it.** Resolve avoidable messes and use remaining political capital to help colleagues.
- **A replacement is a future member of your network.** Transfer the tacit knowledge that would otherwise make their first months needlessly difficult.
- **The final scene shapes memory.** A concise, positive exit story becomes the durable version others repeat.
- **Moving on starts with stories, not job requests.** Curiosity-led conversations open the hidden job market and can evolve into offers.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
Prerequisites: try redesign, ask “What am I doing wrong?”, choose the quit deliberately, and ideally secure the next role.

Then execute four steps:

1. **Improve the campsite:** complete commitments, fix known issues, and advocate for colleagues.
2. **Rev the network:** reconnect with valuable coworkers and meet people you should know before leaving.
3. **Set up the replacement:** write a concise reference manual covering recurring work, helpers, live issues, dormant risks, procedures, and direct-report strengths.
4. **Exit well:** give adequate notice and repeat a positive story about the opportunity ahead, the value of the old role, and the people staying.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Plan the transition over two to six months when circumstances allow; keep delivering during the search.
- Tell only people who need to know before the resignation is public.
- Use a Take It with Me inventory of skills, relationships, lessons, credibility, and accomplishments.
- Seek prototype conversations without a résumé; ask for a person's story and end by asking what they would do next.
- After several genuine conversations in a compelling organization, ask what steps would be involved in exploring a fit.
- Never use the exit story to settle scores; document legitimate misconduct through appropriate channels separately.

## Notable examples
Before leaving Apple, Bill Burnett spent months arranging promotions for two deserving team members and handing a major laptop program to a trusted leader. He departed after seven years with the team strengthened and his network intact, then moved into a design consultancy.

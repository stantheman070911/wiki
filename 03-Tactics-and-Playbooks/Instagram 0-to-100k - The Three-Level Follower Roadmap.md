# Instagram 0-to-100k - The Three-Level Follower Roadmap

## One-line summary
Growth from 0 to 100k splits into three levels — the brutal 0–1k setup grind, the 1k–10k study-and-double-down phase, and the 10k–100k "just repeat" phase — each running the same script-film-edit-upload loop with a shifting content mix.

## Context
Most accounts die in the first level because 0–1k is the hardest, least-rewarded stretch. Knowing the level you're in tells you exactly what to optimize, so you neither over-engineer early nor coast late.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
**Level 1 — 0 to 1,000 followers (setup + grind).** The hardest level; expect ~50–100 videos to break 1k (brand-new accounts take longer — the creator's established account took ~2 weeks, a brand-new one ~3 months). Steps:
1. **Personal-brand basics** — the "house": niche + 5–7 subniches, three content pillars, and a branding/editing style. (See [[The Personal-Brand House - Foundation, Interior, Exterior]].)
2. **Optimize the profile** (wrong → right): **one** bio link, not a Linktree (multiple links can cost ~50% of conversions); **username** = your real or business name; **tagline** = name + a tagline line + a searchable keyword (niche or occupation); **PFP** = crystal-clear close-up on a solid background (tool: PFP Maker, matched to brand colors); **bio** = a clean 4-line framework stating who you are and what you do in ~5 seconds.
3. **Set posting amount and schedule** (easy/moderate/hard) — mainly for accountability; specific days don't matter.
4. **Write scripts** using the seven factors. (See [[The Seven Factors of a Short - Topic to Editing]].)
5. **Film**, 6. **Edit**, 7. **Upload**, 8. **Repeat** steps 4–7, batching a week to a month of content at a time until 1k.

**Level 2 — 1,000 to 10,000 (study + double down + mix).**
1. **Study** your five best and five worst videos (Instagram's Edits app shows the analytics).
2. **Double down** on your outliers (templatizing). (See [[Double-Down and the 70-20-10 Rule - Templatizing Viral Outliers]].)
3. Apply the **70/20/10 rule** to each month's batch (see [[Double-Down and the 70-20-10 Rule - Templatizing Viral Outliers]]).
4. Script-film-edit-upload on repeat until 10k.

**Level 3 — 10,000 to 100,000 (repeat).** Essentially identical to Level 2 — keep running script-film-edit-upload under the 70/20/10 mix every month until 100k. The differentiator here is building a *personal brand*, not a following.

## Tactics / how to apply
- **Filming ops:** use a phone + an inexpensive tripod + a clip-on mic (no fancy camera needed); film one script bullet at a time and let the editor cut the pauses.
- **Script organization for filming/editing:** put the format at the top; black bullets = the spoken script, red bullets = the shot/action for that line, green bullets = notes telling the editor what to add on that line.
- **File hack:** one folder per content batch, a subfolder per short; upload clips *between* shots so nothing needs re-organizing at the end.
- **Editing:** hire an editor if you can (business owners especially); otherwise CapCut is enough to start.
- **Caption structure:** line 1 = the written hook, line 2 = the CTA, line 3 = 3–5 keyword hashtags (treat hashtags as keywords that help reach the right people, not as a performance lever).
- Batch a week-to-a-month of scripts at a time, sized to your posting schedule; never more than a month ahead.

## Notable examples
- The creator's own accounts: an established account reached 1k in ~2 weeks; a brand-new account took ~3 months — new accounts are harder to grow early.
- Typical range to 1k: ~50–100 videos, shorter or longer per person.

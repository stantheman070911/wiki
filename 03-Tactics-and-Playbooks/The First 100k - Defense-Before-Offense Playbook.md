# The First 100k - Defense-Before-Offense Playbook

## One-line summary
When you're broke with no skills, play total defense — slash spending to build a runway — so you can move from survival mode into thrive mode and start making bets.

## Context
This is Hormozi's "brutally honest advice for poor people" with no skill set yet. The goal is to cut survival cost as low as possible so you can think long-term and afford to make bets on education, opportunities, and experiments.

## Key insights
- **Survival mode vs. thrive mode.** If you make $40k/mo and spend $40k, you're in survival mode just like someone making $4k. Spend a fraction of what you make and each month buys many months of runway; runway is what lets you think long-term and take bets.
- **The first $100k is the hardest** and there's no single secret — it's brute force, saved "$1 at a time." Later you can move decimals by selling expensive things, but that requires becoming a different person first.
- **Every hour worked pays twice:** you make money and you're not spending money. Working more is a net positive in both directions.
- **Impress the right audience.** To impress poor people, outspend them; to impress rich people, outwork them. Leasing the Ferrari only signals to those below you; the rich know what it costs and see wasted reinvestment.
- **Your lifestyle is your competitor's opportunity.** Live far below your means so you have capital ready when bigger, higher-zero opportunities appear. "There's zero advantage to being accurately judged" — if strong, appear modest.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
**The very-broke defensive moves (only if you have no skill set yet):**
1. Stop eating out — discount groceries only.
2. Stop buying new clothes — Goodwill only.
3. Only attend *free* friend events (and if they don't support your goals, they aren't friends).
4. Use remaining time to (a) apply to higher-paying jobs, (b) pick up side-hustle work, (c) learn skills that trade for a higher hourly rate.
5. Find the hardest worker in the room and double their input (they knock 100 doors, you knock 200).
Then: **wait ~36 months** — patient with outputs, impatient with inputs.

*Caveat:* once you have a base skill set that earns (e.g., $500/30 min selling), the calculus flips — outsource anything below your hourly rate so you can keep earning and learning.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Take all excess above bare survival and reinvest it into skills, experiments, and bets — you won't save your way to wealth on minimum wage.
- Treat failed experiments as experience points, not zeros; you're always at "where you were + N experiences."
- Use cash-only budgeting to feel spending (Hormozi lived on a fixed cash amount from one client's monthly payment).

## Notable examples
- Hormozi's neighbor Jacob was expected to do $100/day; told to double the hardest worker, he did $300–400/day and got better faster.
- Hormozi cleaned, billed, sold, taught, and fixed equipment himself at his first gym because he didn't know he could hire — using what he had.

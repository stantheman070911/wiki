# The Ackerman Bargaining System - Calibrated Concessions and Exact Numbers

## One-line summary
Set a target, anchor at 65 percent, and move through shrinking 85–95–100 percent concessions while using empathy, calibrated refusals, and an exact final number.

## Context
Once discovery and rapport reach price, anxiety often pushes both sides into arbitrary concessions or a reflexive midpoint. The Ackerman system supplies a prepared concession path that makes movement deliberate while preserving the relationship.

## Key insights
- **The target is set before pressure begins.** Preparation prevents the first extreme offer from becoming the new reality.
- **A strong opening anchor creates room.** The initial 65 percent offer is intentionally extreme but must still be deliverable with calm empathy.
- **Concessions shrink.** Moving to 85, 95, and finally 100 percent signals that the limit is approaching.
- **Every increase must purchase movement.** Calibrated questions and gentle forms of “No” should prompt the counterpart to bid before you concede.
- **Exact numbers imply calculation.** A nonround final figure feels less provisional than a round estimate.
- **A noncash final item marks exhaustion.** Adding something of little monetary value signals that no price room remains.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
For a buyer with target price **T**:

1. Establish the best outcome, walk-away point, market evidence, and noncash variables.
2. Open at **0.65T**.
3. After counterpart movement, advance to **0.85T**.
4. Repeat the exchange and advance to **0.95T**.
5. Finish at **1.00T**, expressed as a precise number, and add a small nonmonetary term.

For a seller, apply the same diminishing-concession logic in the opposite direction from an ambitious opening anchor toward the target.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Script several respectful refusals in advance: “How am I supposed to do that?”, “That is generous, but it does not work for me,” and “I’m sorry, no.”
- Never improve your own offer merely to break silence; require new information or counterpart movement.
- Use calibrated questions to pivot from a hostile price anchor to objectives and terms.
- Make each concession visibly difficult through patience and smaller increments, without theatrical deception.
- Write the exact final number before the negotiation so stress cannot round it upward.
- Preserve a ready-to-walk mindset; the system is not permission to cross a bad-deal boundary.

## Notable examples
A student facing a rent increase researched comparable apartments, used an extreme opening anchor, moved through smaller concessions, and ended at the precise figure of $1,829 per month. The landlord accepted even though the original renewal proposal had been $2,000–$2,100.

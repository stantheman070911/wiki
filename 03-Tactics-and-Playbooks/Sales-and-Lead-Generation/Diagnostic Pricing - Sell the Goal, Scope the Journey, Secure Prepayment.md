# Diagnostic Pricing - Sell the Goal, Scope the Journey, Secure Prepayment

## One-line summary
Price a transformation by diagnosing the gap, translating it into a credible journey, and selling the completed goal—not a bundle of hours whose value falls as the provider improves.

## Context
Hourly and session-based pricing makes buyers compare inputs while penalizing the provider for speed. Diagnostic pricing begins with the customer's current state and target, then scopes the work required to cross the gap.

## Key insights
- **Sell the goal, not the technician's time.** The desired outcome is the value-bearing unit.
- **A credible timeline makes price legible.** Break the journey into phases rather than making an unsupported promise.
- **Prepayment aligns commitment and cash flow.** Discounts or guarantees can be made conditional on paying ahead.
- **Downsell payment structure before outcome.** Preserve the scope when possible by changing deposit and installment timing.
- **Guarantees work best when tied to customer behavior.** Reward completion or adherence rather than promising an uncontrollable result.
- **The gap makes price comparable to value.** Quantify the difference between current and future states, then compare the full cost of change with the business value of closing that gap.
- **A price objection may reveal weak discovery.** If the buyer cannot connect the offer to a measured outcome, defending features or discounting will not repair the missing value case.
- **Budget and affordability are different constraints.** “Not budgeted” means funds exist but were allocated elsewhere and can be reconsidered; “cannot afford it” means the buyer lacks access to the funds and may need to be disqualified.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
1. Measure the starting point and define the target.
2. Estimate a defensible rate of progress.
3. Add maintenance, recovery, or consolidation phases that reality requires.
4. Multiply the full journey by the program's weekly or phase value.
5. Present full prepayment first; offer a smaller prepayment discount or split terms second.
6. If affordability remains the issue, use a seesaw: ask for the largest feasible deposit to minimize later payments.
7. When price is challenged, restate the agreed current state, future state, and cost of delay; ask the buyer to explain why the outcome is not worth the investment before changing the price.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Use an assessment or calculator during the sales conversation so scope follows diagnosis.
- State the assumptions behind timeline and price.
- Offer a prepayment incentive only when cash now is more valuable than the discount.
- Make any refund contingent on defined participation, reporting, or milestone behavior.
- Position the seller as the architect of the plan, not merely the person performing individual sessions.
- Do not confuse value with pressure: if the verified gap is too small or the buyer genuinely cannot afford the change, walk away rather than manufacturing urgency.

## Notable examples
For weight loss, Hormozi maps the difference between current and target weight to realistic loss and maintenance phases, then prices the entire journey. For personal training, he recommends a goal-based three-month package rather than isolated hours, with part of the price returned only if the client follows the process and reaches the agreed result.

# Cold Outreach - Personalize, Big Fast Value, Volume

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[03-Tactics-and-Playbooks/Tactics and Playbooks Index|Tactics and Playbooks]] → [[03-Tactics-and-Playbooks/Sales-and-Lead-Generation/Sales and Lead Generation Map|Sales and Lead Generation]]

## One-line summary
Reaching strangers one-to-one works when you solve three problems in order — get a way to contact them, make a cold message feel warm through personalization plus big, fast, free value, and then do enough volume, follow-up, and automation that the right people can't help but respond.

## Context
Cold outreach is the advanced cousin of warm outreach and one of the [[The Core Four and the Rule of 100 - Lead Generation Fundamentals|Core Four]] channels. Reach for it when you want faster growth or more *predictable* lead flow than warm outreach and content give you. Its defining constraint is trust: strangers give you far less time and need far more incentive to move toward you. It is private (invisible to competitors), reliable (inputs map to outputs), and durable (platform rules for calls/email/DMs barely change).

## Framework / model
### The three problems, solved in order
1. **Get a way to contact them** — build/buy/scrape a list of the most qualified strangers with real contact points.
2. **Figure out what to say** — personalize, then lead with big fast value (below).
3. **Get enough attention to engage** — volume, automation, and follow-up (below).

### Making a cold message land: personalize + big fast value
- **Personalize (make cold look warm).** Find 1–3 things a friend might know about them, compliment it, and ideally show how it benefited you, before you get to your ask. People give more time to someone who clearly researched them. Writing the whole template *below a third-grade reading level* lifted responses ~50%.
- **Big fast value.** Strangers give you ~30 seconds — don't tickle interest, blow minds. Offer/lead magnet must be *big* and *fast*: give away something people *actually pay for* (not merely "should"). When Hormozi's team swapped a "game-planning session" lead magnet for as much free service as they could afford, take rates roughly 3×'d.

### Getting attention at scale: volume, automation, follow-up
- **Automate delivery** where a machine can convey the message (pre-recorded voicemail/DM/video, templated email/text) — trades some response rate for far more reach; you have near-infinite strangers, so burning a list matters less.
- **Automate distribution** (dialers, blasts) — generally trade personalization for scale; the fewer, higher-value the leads, the more you personalize (hedge-fund managers: personalize all; broad consumer: less).
- **Follow up more times, more ways.** Contact each lead multiple times across multiple channels; a non-response on one channel is a reason to follow up on another ("calling to follow up on my email"). After exhausting the list, wait 3–6 months and run it again — circumstances and timing change.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Batch research first, then open every message with the personalized hook; keep phone/chat scripts to 1–2 pages and cold emails to half a page — no awards for prettiest script.
- Lead with a lead magnet or offer that is genuinely something people pay for; if response is weak, up the ante on the *value* before tweaking anything else.
- Run the Rule of 100: at least 100 outreach actions/day for 100 days before judging the channel; count in hundreds because it is a volume game.
- Give each rep a *reduced* lead load (~70% of capacity) so leads get worked fully, and set absolute quotas ("70 leads → 7 appointments").
- Track every step of the funnel obsessively; you win when cost-to-acquire is at most one-third of a customer's lifetime gross profit (aim far higher — see [[Client-Financed Acquisition - Make the Customer Fund the Next Customer]]).
- Never contact someone who asked you to stop — turning neutrals into yeses is more efficient than fighting a negative, and reputation follows you.

## Notable examples
- A software firm in Hormozi's own niche ran **100% cold outreach** across a 30-person team to ~$10M/month — invisible to him until a rejected salesman mentioned it. Building the channel from scratch took his team ~9 months (Sept 0 sales → the next May ~$480K/month), despite an optimistic 12-week estimate — cold outreach is slow to stand up but brutally reliable once built.
- A portfolio company automated scrape→craft→send: one VA sending ~2,000 emails/day generated ~40 engaged leads/day, ~4 new customers/day at ~$20K each — enough to blow past 100 customers/month.

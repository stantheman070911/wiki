# Affiliate Armies - Recruiting Businesses to Sell for You

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[03-Tactics-and-Playbooks/Tactics and Playbooks Index|Tactics and Playbooks]] → [[03-Tactics-and-Playbooks/Sales-and-Lead-Generation/Sales and Lead Generation Map|Sales and Lead Generation]]

## One-line summary
An affiliate is an independent business that sells your stuff to *its* warm audience for a commission — so instead of selling customers one at a time, you sell affiliates once and each becomes a recurring, compounding stream of leads.

## Context
Affiliates are the highest-leverage of the four lead-getters (customers, employees, agencies, affiliates) in [[The Core Four and the Rule of 100 - Lead Generation Fundamentals]]. They differ from referrals: an affiliate runs its own advertising and agrees to offer your product to its engaged leads. The unique demand is that your offer *to the affiliate* isn't your product — it's a fast, simple, low-effort way for them to make commissions. Done right, one super-affiliate that recruits sub-affiliates gives leads that climb every month with no further work from you.

## Framework / model
### The six-step build
1. **Find ideal affiliates** — businesses whose warm audience is full of people like your customers. Ask "who already has my leads?" and sort into categories: softwares, products, equipment, services, groups they belong to, events they attend. A business hitting multiple categories is a strong bet.
2. **Make them an offer** — advertise it like any offer (call-out → value → CTA), but the value is "make more money from your current customers, with high likelihood, without building/delivering/supporting the product, starting tomorrow."
3. **Qualify them (get them invested)** — make them a *customer* (they buy/use the product) and/or an *expert* (they pay for onboarding/certification). If they won't buy it, they shouldn't sell it. Charge ~10–20% of what an average active affiliate earns in year one.
4. **Decide what to pay** — pay for new customers (and, if you track it, earlier steps like appointments). Set payout off your **maximum allowable CAC**, then use a **three-tier structure** so the blended payout runs below your max: Tier 1 (~25% CAC on sign-up) · Tier 2 (~50% on activation) · Tier 3 (~100% on sustained performance). Pay with *product* at low tiers where possible ("sell three, get it free").
5. **Get them advertising (launch)** — activate with a big launch using [[The Whisper-Tease-Shout Launch]]; nothing activates affiliates like a launch plus cash.
6. **Keep them advertising (integrate)** — turn a one-time sale into enduring flow via one of three integrations (below).

### Three integration depths (easiest → hardest)
1. **Affiliate gives your lead magnet away** with every purchase of *their* stuff (makes their offer more valuable; you upsell the leads).
2. **Affiliate sells your lead magnet** (they keep the cash — pure profit, no work for them; you make money on the upsell to your core offer).
3. **Affiliate sells your core offer** directly (they take a larger share of lifetime gross profit; you just deliver).
> **White-label** the lead magnets you already made for your own audience so affiliates can deploy them with a logo swap.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Build the affiliate hit-list from your best customers' surrounding ecosystem; call customers and ask "who else do you buy from / work for?" if stuck.
- Get affiliates invested up front — a bulk purchase or paid certification both funds acquisition *and* means they'll actually pay attention.
- Design payout tiers so top performers earn the full CAC while the blended average stays below it; bank the difference for contests, recruiting, or profit.
- Reward performance with product (retail-valued, cost-priced to you) rather than cash at lower volumes — a value arbitrage both sides like.
- Measure returns as **cost-to-acquire-an-affiliate vs. gross profit of all customers that affiliate sends** (aim ≥3:1; the worked example lands ~12.5:1). To improve: lower affiliate CAC (better offer), raise LTGP via a better launch, or raise it via a better integration.

## Notable examples
- **Prestige Labs** launched flat (~$150K in 3 weeks against $3M inventory), then week four hit ~$450K and stayed there — ~300 orders/day across ~400 affiliates, with zero paid ads or sales team; the gym affiliates did all selling.
- **Allan** grew through three affiliate tiers (super-affiliates → agencies → local businesses → end-consumer leads) to ~$1.7M/month within 6 months; each super-affiliate compounded ($12.5K month one, $25K month two, and so on).
- **Employers as affiliates:** giving every new hire at a company a free service (e.g., a massage in the onboarding packet) turns non-competing businesses into easy lead sources.

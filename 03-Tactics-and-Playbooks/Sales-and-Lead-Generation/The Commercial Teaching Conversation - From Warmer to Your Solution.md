# The Commercial Teaching Conversation - From Warmer to Your Solution

## One-line summary
Lead customers from a familiar business challenge to a surprising reframe, make its consequences rationally and emotionally unavoidable, establish a new way to act, and only then introduce your solution.

## Context
Most pitch decks lead with the supplier’s mission, capabilities, logos, footprint, and differentiators before the customer has a reason to care. An insight-led conversation reverses the order so supplier relevance becomes the conclusion of a valuable customer story.

## Key insights
- **Lead to, not with.** The supplier should enter near the end of the story; the customer’s business remains the subject until the need for a solution has been established.
- **Hypotheses outperform blank-slate discovery.** Arriving with an informed view of likely challenges respects the customer’s time and demonstrates preparation.
- **A reframe is a headline, not the proof.** Its purpose is to create thoughtful surprise and curiosity before the evidence arrives.
- **Logic creates conviction; narrative creates ownership.** Numbers establish scale, while a familiar story prevents the customer from dismissing the issue as someone else’s problem.
- **Customers buy the solution before your solution.** They must first agree that a different behavior or capability is necessary.
- **Design backward from the unique strength.** Without a defined destination, the pitch can create demand that any competitor can capture.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
The six-step choreography is:

1. **Warmer:** present two or three likely challenges seen in similar companies and invite correction.
2. **Reframe:** reveal the unexpected connection, overlooked cost, or larger opportunity.
3. **Rational Drowning:** quantify the consequences of the status quo or value of the reframe.
4. **Emotional Impact:** tell a recognizable story that makes the customer see themselves in the problem.
5. **A New Way:** define the capabilities and behavior required to solve it without mentioning your offering yet.
6. **Your Solution:** show why your distinctive capabilities enable that new way better than alternatives.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Build in the order 6 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5 → 1: define the unique destination, find the belief blocking it, prove the cost, personalize it, describe the new behavior, then select the familiar opening challenges.
- Audit the first five slides of current collateral; move supplier-centered material behind the insight journey or remove it.
- Test the reframe for the reaction “I had not thought about it that way,” not enthusiastic agreement with a known problem.
- Calculate ROI on fixing the newly exposed problem before showing ROI on purchasing the product.
- Pressure-test the idea with the SAFE–BOLD dimensions: big, outperforming, leading-edge, and difficult enough that help is valuable.
- Rehearse transitions so the solution feels like the natural consequence of the story rather than a sudden pitch.

## Notable examples
ADP Dealer Services taught dealerships that buying isolated low-cost software systems created duplicated processes and hidden expenses—up to 40 percent redundant costs. Its Profit Clinics spent the first two-thirds on dealership economics and only then showed how an integrated ADP system enabled the new way.

# The Challenger Selling Model - Teach, Tailor, and Take Control

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[03-Tactics-and-Playbooks/Tactics and Playbooks Index|Tactics and Playbooks]] → [[03-Tactics-and-Playbooks/Sales-and-Lead-Generation/Sales and Lead Generation Map|Sales and Lead Generation]]

## One-line summary
In complex sales, outperform by teaching a differentiated perspective, tailoring it to each stakeholder’s economics, and taking respectful control of progress and value.

## Context
As suppliers moved from products to complex solutions, customers responded with more consensus requirements, risk aversion, customization demands, and third-party scrutiny. Traditional discovery and relationship-building alone became less able to create demand or overcome the status quo.

## Key insights
- **Five recurring rep profiles appear in the research:** Hard Worker, Relationship Builder, Lone Wolf, Reactive Problem Solver, and Challenger. These are behavioral clusters, not fixed personalities.
- **Complexity changes what wins.** Hard work can dominate simple transactional sales, but Challengers represented more than half of star performers in complex solution environments.
- **Relationships are an earned result, not the primary cause.** Customers reward reps who create value by improving their thinking; likability without progress is insufficient.
- **The three skills are interdependent.** Teaching without tailoring is irrelevant, tailoring without teaching is undifferentiated, and taking control without creating value is merely annoying.
- **Constructive tension is the medium.** Challengers keep useful discomfort alive long enough for customers to reconsider assumptions and act.
- **The model is learnable behavior.** It does not require a wholesale personality change, but it does require tools, coaching, and organizational support.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
The **Teach–Tailor–Take Control** model:

1. **Teach for differentiation:** offer a perspective that changes how the customer sees an economic problem or opportunity and leads toward a distinctive supplier capability.
2. **Tailor for resonance:** translate the insight into the business outcomes, value drivers, and language of each stakeholder.
3. **Take control:** maintain momentum, hold firm on value, and press respectfully through resistance or indecision.

Use the full model for complex, disruptive, multi-stakeholder sales; do not overinvest in it where success is mostly a function of transactional volume.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Classify current rep behavior from observed calls rather than quota attainment; a high performer may be a Lone Wolf rather than a Challenger.
- For each complex opportunity, verify that the rep can state the teaching insight, stakeholder-specific outcomes, and next powerful request.
- Coach the three skills as a combination and role-play the constructive tension between customer comfort and necessary change.
- Build rep capability and organizational assets in parallel: insight creation, messaging, stakeholder maps, coaching, and reinforcement.
- Pilot with willing reps, capture core-performer success stories, and use nearby peer examples to cross adoption plateaus.

## Notable examples
Relationship Builders sought entry into the customer’s comfort zone; Challengers sought to move the customer beyond it. The research found nearly 40 percent of all star performers in the Challenger cluster, compared with only 7 percent in the Relationship Builder cluster, with the gap becoming more extreme in complex sales.

# The Digital-Economy Income Shift - IP, Media, Data, and Code

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[05-Intelligence-and-Research/Intelligence and Research Index|Intelligence and Research]]

## One-line summary
Digital markets can increase the advantage of people who combine measurable performance with portable assets—intellectual property, media, data, and code—while weakening some geographic wage premiums, but the timing and political consequences remain uncertain.

## Context
This entry separates a durable strategic observation from speculative macro forecasts. Priestley describes the emerging asset mix; Davidson and Rees-Mogg provide an earlier mechanism based on lower communication costs, global competition, and credible exit. Claims about exact wage shares, universal basic income, state decline, wealth taxes, and frictionless jurisdictional mobility are source opinions, not established outcomes.

## Key insights
- **Economic advantage follows the era's controlling assets.** Land dominated agricultural systems, factories and offices dominated industrial systems, and reusable knowledge plus digital distribution increasingly shape modern businesses.
- **Digital markets often follow power laws.** Search, media, software, and global expert categories can direct a large share of demand toward a small number of visible winners.
- **A narrow category can make top-tier performance achievable.** Define a valuable intersection where genuine expertise and proof can place the person near the top, then widen the category only after establishing authority.
- **Income can be separated into wages, benefits, performance fees, and asset yield.** Automation pressure is greatest where work is average, repeatable, and easily specified, while accountable judgment and measurable outcomes may retain pricing power.
- **Portable digital assets amplify performance.** IP, content libraries, customer data, software, and a trusted personal brand can attract demand and improve delivery across geography.
- **Portability changes bargaining power.** When income is less tied to one employer or place, a skilled person can compare customers, firms, and jurisdictions; the value comes from a credible outside option, not mobility for its own sake.
- **Digital competition can narrow geographic gaps while widening performance gaps.** Similar skills can reach similar markets from more places, while global discovery can direct disproportionate rewards to a small number of visible specialists.
- **Portability is not immunity.** Tax, labor, privacy, platform, and residency rules can change; digital owners still face regulation, concentration risk, and dependency on infrastructure they do not control.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
Audit a career or business across four digital assets:

1. **IP:** named methods, expertise, cases, rights, and reusable decisions.
2. **Media:** trusted content and owned audience relationships.
3. **Data:** permissioned customer knowledge and feedback that improve decisions.
4. **Code:** software and automation that deliver value repeatedly.

Then ask which income depends on average time, which depends on measurable performance, and which continues because an asset remains useful.

Add a portability test: identify which assets, customer relationships, licenses, family obligations, and legal rules can actually move. A theoretical ability to work anywhere has little negotiating value when distribution, residency, or critical infrastructure remains concentrated.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Define a narrow, valuable category where proof—not merely self-description—supports a top-tier claim.
- Convert repeated expert work into documented IP and software-assisted workflows.
- Build owned audience and customer relationships rather than depending entirely on a platform feed.
- Treat data as permissioned infrastructure with governance, not as a pile of contacts.
- Increase performance-linked income gradually while preserving a financial runway through the transition.
- Scenario-plan regulation, platform loss, relocation constraints, and automation risk instead of assuming digital assets are untouchable.
- Build at least one legitimate alternate customer, channel, or operating location before treating exit as bargaining power.

## Notable examples
Priestley contrasts an industrial-era “average worker” with a globally discoverable specialist at the intersection of executive fitness and first-marathon coaching. Narrowing the game changes the competitive set; it does not eliminate the need to become genuinely good.

# ChatGPT Advertising - Intent, Memory, and Human Escalation

## One-line summary
If conversational AI becomes an advertising surface, its advantage could be unusually rich intent and context, but advertisers should prepare measurable economics and useful escalation paths rather than assume the forecast or channel performance is proven.

## Context
This is a source-attributed forecast, not a verified description of product availability, targeting policy, privacy design, or results. Its durable value is a preparation framework for any conversational channel that can infer the user's active job to be done.

## Key insights
- **Conversation can reveal active intent.** A user's questions may describe a problem, time horizon, constraints, language, and stage of decision more directly than a passive feed interaction.
- **Context could improve relevance and increase privacy risk.** Memory and personalization may make recommendations more useful, but implementation details determine whether the channel is acceptable and trustworthy.
- **Economics should set the budget.** An allowable cost per lead and sale supports scaling while results remain inside the threshold; a fixed annual budget obscures marginal performance.
- **The creative fundamentals remain stable.** Hook, value proposition, credibility, and call to action still determine whether attention becomes action.
- **The CTA must be a genuine progression.** Useful moves include speaking with an accountable human, entering a specialized workflow, or completing an appropriate transaction—not simply leaving one generic chatbot for another.
- **Personal evidence differentiates commoditized information.** Lived experience, reputation, and accountable judgment can matter more when generic explanations are abundant.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
Prepare a channel-agnostic conversational-ad test:

1. Calculate allowable cost per sale and derive an initial allowable cost per qualified lead.
2. Write hooks around the user's pain, desired prize, or next process step.
3. State the specific value and the evidence behind it.
4. Prepare multiple CTAs: human consultation, specialized tool or assessment, and low-friction transaction where appropriate.
5. Run a limited test only when the platform, policy, consent model, and measurement are clear.
6. Scale from observed qualified-customer economics, not audience size forecasts.

## Tactics / how to apply
- Separate assumptions about future inventory from verified platform capabilities.
- Design landing pages and assessments now so the business can test any new intent-rich channel quickly.
- Track qualified leads, sales, retention, and customer quality rather than cheap clicks.
- Ensure the destination delivers more specialization, responsibility, or human judgment than the source conversation.
- Maintain a personal-brand evidence layer—cases, published thinking, and third-party proof—that both humans and recommendation systems can evaluate.

## Notable examples
Priestley compares the possible opening with early search and social advertising, when large attention pools initially had limited advertiser competition. The analogy suggests a testable opportunity, not guaranteed channel economics.

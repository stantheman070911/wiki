# Personal-Brand Positioning and the No-Competition Model

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[02-Social-Media-Strategy/Social Media Strategy Index|Social Media Strategy]]

## One-line summary
Almost no one actually competes with you — of 100 who say they'll start, ~3 remain — so pick a niche whose "content TAM" serves your goal and stand out with one of three positioning moves.

## Context
Two questions decide whether a personal brand cuts through: *where do I aim* (content-market fit) and *how do I stand out* (positioning). This entry captures both, plus the attrition model that explains why standing out is more available than it feels.

## Key insights
- **There's no competition.** Of 100 people who say they'll start a brand in your niche: ~80 never start, ~10 quit early, ~5 catch shiny-object syndrome, and of the last 5, ~2 are unlucky (life happens) — leaving ~3, and you can be one. In most niches the aspirant pool is small; simply persisting past ~60 days puts you near the top. (Heuristic, not a measured funnel — huge niches leave a bigger surviving pool.)
- **Content-market fit = niche-maxing × content TAM.** *Niche-maxing* gets you in front of a hyper-specific audience — low views, but views that matter to your goal. *Content TAM* is how many people are interested in the topic at all. Choose niches by whether their TAM actually advances your objective; a small-TAM, ultra-high-value niche (e.g. "the math of money") can be perfect if the goal is career leverage.
- **Know your goal.** The three common ones: (1) get hired / paid more, (2) build distribution for side projects and freelance, (3) attract a partner. Positioning and topic choice flow from which goal you're serving.
- **View quality over viral.** Viral ≠ money or value; a few thousand of the right people beat vanity reach.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
**The three positioning moves:**
1. **Counter-position** — flip the script on what's popular in the niche. Lever on production level, experience level, or claim ("they say it's easy, you say it's hard"). Example: a young creator's low-fi "$40k month" relatability counter-positioned against established, high-production names.
2. **Mirror with a twist** — cover the same content as the niche but radically change delivery/production/aesthetic, so you're not a bland copycat. Example: written playbooks re-done on a whiteboard; an agency framing everything through "cult" metaphors (Cult Holdings).
3. **Standout demographic** — a different angle by gender, location, lifestyle, or subculture. Example: Shelby Sap doing sales training for women in a previously bro-dominated niche.

## Tactics / how to apply
- List the topics you're genuinely excited to talk about (personal and business), estimate each one's content TAM, and keep those whose TAM serves your goal.
- Before publishing a series, pick one of the three positioning moves deliberately rather than defaulting to "mirror" (straight copying invites "why are you taking this from X?" comments and caps you).
- Remember the attrition model when discouraged early: persistence past the first ~60 days already beats the vast majority.

## Notable examples
- The creator's own observation: marketing/branding has near-infinite demand on YouTube yet only a handful of creators above ~50k — proof the "no competition" model holds in practice.
- Shelby Sap's women-focused sales-training angle as a clean "standout demographic" play.

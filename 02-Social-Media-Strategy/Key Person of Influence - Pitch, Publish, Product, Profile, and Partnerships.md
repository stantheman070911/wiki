# Key Person of Influence - Pitch, Publish, Product, Profile, and Partnerships

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[02-Social-Media-Strategy/Social Media Strategy Index|Social Media Strategy]]

## One-line summary
Moving from skilled worker to industry authority requires five leverage skills beyond technical competence: pitching ideas, publishing thinking, productizing value, building a visible profile, and forming multiplier partnerships.

## Context
Digital markets distribute attention and income through power laws. Becoming incrementally better at delivery may improve a worker's pay, but it rarely creates the visibility, assets, and alliances that concentrate opportunity at the top of a category.

## Key insights
- **Pitching enrolls people into a future.** Social pitches spark curiosity, scheduled pitches change a course of action, and sales pitches secure a transaction.
- **Publishing converts private knowledge into public authority.** Books, reports, videos, podcasts, and posts prove sustained care about a subject and let ideas travel without the author.
- **Products free value from time.** A gift creates attention, a product for prospects creates trust, a core offer creates transformation, and a product for clients sustains the relationship.
- **Profile is how value appears from a distance.** Social accounts, awards and associations, live events, and third-party platforms create the evidence people find before deciding to engage.
- **Partnerships multiply existing strengths.** Brand, product, and distribution partnerships can create an outcome neither party could produce alone.
- **The goal is to become the spotlight.** A useful personal brand points attention toward ideas, customers, and outcomes rather than making private life the product.

## Framework / model (if applicable)
Audit the **Five Ps**:

1. **Pitch:** Can you explain who you help, the important problem, your method, and the bigger mission?
2. **Publish:** Is your best thinking findable in reusable public artifacts?
3. **Product:** Can people progress from free value to a transformation and an ongoing relationship?
4. **Profile:** Do search results and third-party signals make your relevance and credibility obvious?
5. **Partnership:** Which established brand, complementary product, or distribution owner could multiply the result?

## Tactics / how to apply
- Write and rehearse social, scheduled, and transactional versions of the pitch.
- Publish directionally correct work at sufficient volume; let audience response reveal which ideas deserve refinement.
- Replace bespoke time sales with a documented product ecosystem.
- Search your own name and repair ambiguity, weak evidence, or inconsistent positioning.
- Pursue one partnership in each category: brand, product, and distribution.
- Use client outcomes as profile evidence so authority grows with customer success.

## Notable examples
Priestley contrasts a worker who repeatedly changes industries to chase visible winners with one who stays in a valued field and develops the five leverage skills. The first repeatedly returns to beginner status; the second moves toward the power-law edge of the same field.

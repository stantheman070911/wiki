# Creator Vision Framework: What x Who x Why

> **Up:** [[Home|Home]] → [[02-Social-Media-Strategy/Social Media Strategy Index|Social Media Strategy]]

## One-line summary
Your personal brand sits at the intersection of What (message and content pillars), Who (target avatar), and Why (your story) — "become the niche" instead of niching down to one topic.



## Context

This page organizes **Creator Vision Framework: What x Who x Why** as a reusable strategy; the sections below preserve its original framework, steps, and examples.
## Core thesis
A personal brand is simply how the world views you — the modern equivalent of a reputation that earns trust and attracts opportunity. Content creation is treated as a science and a learnable skill, not spontaneous creativity: the best creators reuse identifiable patterns rather than reinventing the wheel.

## Become the niche (don't niche down)
Problem with traditional niching: committing to one topic limits your ability to evolve, and audiences disengage when you shift interests. Alternative: become the niche yourself. Benefits: longevity, flexibility, versatility. Note: this is about *topic breadth under one personal identity*, not about audience targeting — it doesn't contradict narrowing your target *customer* (see [[受眾定位與先小後大法則]]); you can have one narrow target avatar while covering multiple facets of your own life as content.

## Branch 1 — What (message + content pillars)
Your overarching message ties all content together so it stays varied yet familiar. Three questions to find your message:
1. Do I hold a contrarian view? (Does my industry/society say one thing while I believe another?)
2. What change do I want my audience to experience?
3. Where do people naturally turn to me for help? (What they *actually* come to you for, not what you wish they did.)

**Four content pillars** (covering all facets of your life):

| Pillar | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Skill | What you do |
| Mindset | How you think |
| Lifestyle | How you live |
| Passions | What you love |

Passions in particular act as connection points — shared interests build rapport with the audience.

## Branch 2 — Who (target avatar)
- **Demographic** (factual, often visible in platform analytics): age, location, gender, occupation.
- **Psychographic** (how they think and feel): their pains and desires.

Speak descriptively to the psychographic — naming a specific pain stops the scroll. Example: rather than "here's how to make good content," say "if you're a mom struggling with time management, here's a five-minute scripting framework."

## Branch 3 — Why (your story), the most important branch
Sharing your story shifts the audience from championing your tips to championing *you*.

**3P Story Framework:**
1. **Problem** — the struggle you encountered.
2. **Pursuit** — how you overcame it.
3. **Payoff** — the final transformation, lesson, or insight gained.

This is structurally the same pattern as 陳修平's story script formula (成就/概述 → (挫折+轉機)×N → 體悟, see [[短影音文案全公式：標題、開頭、結尾與腳本結構]]) and Hormozi's win narrative (see [[身份、故事與致勝三步]]) — three independent sources converge on the same struggle→overcome→lesson shape for personal storytelling, which is a good signal it's a durable pattern rather than a one-off trick.

## Application
- Write your own What×Who×Why in one sitting: one contrarian belief, one specific avatar (demographic + psychographic pain), and one 3P story draft.
- Before publishing a new content series, check it against your four pillars (skill/mindset/lifestyle/passions) — if everything you post is only "skill," you're likely under-using the other three for connection-building.
- When writing a hook, replace any generic pain statement with the most specific psychographic version you can — name the exact person and exact pain.
